## How to read this document

This is the rulebook for credentialing PioneerOps Operators. Four audiences will use it:

1. **Candidates** preparing for certification — they read it to know exactly what to demonstrate.
2. **Trainers and reviewers** grading certifications — they read it to grade consistently.
3. **The Academy lead** making final certification decisions — they read it for the bar to defend.
4. **Customers and employers** who will eventually rely on the credential — they read it to know what "PioneerOps-certified Operator" actually means.

This document does not teach. It specifies what teaching must produce. Curriculum + lessons + role-play scenarios live elsewhere; this document binds them together into a credential.

## 0. Foundational Principles

Six principles govern every certification decision in this framework. They are not opinions. They are the doctrine in operating form.

### Principle 1 — Certify demonstrated capability, never participation

No certification is ever awarded for attendance, content completion, or seat time. The certifying evidence is *work*: real diagnoses performed, real interventions made, real results verified on real businesses or in real cases.

Knowledge checks (quizzes, lessons) may gate progress along the way; they certify nothing by themselves. A candidate who has completed every lesson but produced no verifiable work cannot pass.

### Principle 2 — Evidence is the instrument of certification

Every certification rests on an **evidence record** — the candidate's documented Loops. Baselines captured, diagnoses with Economic Reality chain complete, actions with pre-declared predictions, verification results including the failures.

An evidence record containing only successes is **disqualifying at every tier**. It reflects either inexperience or curation — both are disqualifying. The Operator who documents their own misses honestly is exhibiting the exact trait the credential is meant to guarantee.

### Principle 3 — The credential is defensible against its weakest holder

A certification is worth what its least capable holder can do. The Academy prefers fewer, harder, slower certifications over volume.

When pressure arises to widen the gate (commercial pressure, hiring pressure, scaling pressure), this section is what's quoted at that meeting. The gate stays.

### Principle 4 — Certification expires

Operational tooling, AI leverage, and the doctrine itself evolve. A 2026 credential asserting 2030 capability is a small fraud the Academy declines to commit.

Certification carries renewal — evidence of continued practice and currency with the evolved standard. Renewal is evidence-based like everything else: show the recent Loops.

### Principle 5 — Tiers mark scope of trust, not accumulation of content

The four tiers exist to answer one market question:

> *What can this person be relied upon to do without supervision?*

Tiers therefore mark expanding scope — from supervised execution to independent customer ownership to mentorship to segment-level authority. Each tier is defined by what the holder has **demonstrated at that scope**, never by hours logged or modules cleared.

### Principle 6 — Demotion is real

A certification reflects current capability. When current capability falls short — through skill atrophy, doctrine drift, repeated evidence-record gaps, or failed renewal — the certification is downgraded or revoked.

Demotion is rare. Demotion is also non-negotiable when warranted. The framework protects the credential, not the holder.

## 1. The Four Tiers — Overview

| Tier | What the title says about the holder |
|---|---|
| **Operator I** | "I can execute the method under supervision on small accounts." |
| **Operator II** | "I can run the full Loop independently on a portfolio of accounts and deliver QBRs to customer CEOs." |
| **Operator III** | "I run strategic accounts, mentor others, and contribute to the doctrine and curriculum." |
| **Executive Advisor** | "I operate as a peer to customer CEOs, set segment-level outcomes, and shape the Academy's doctrine." |

### Tier comparison at a glance

| Dimension | Operator I | Operator II | Operator III | Executive Advisor |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Typical time-in-role to qualify | 30–90 days | 4–9 months | 12–24 months | 24+ months |
| Account scope | 3–5 small | 8–12 mid-tier | 12–18 incl. 2–3 strategic | Segment ownership |
| Supervision required | Weekly + ad-hoc | Biweekly | Monthly | Peer-level |
| Mentorship of others | Not expected | Not expected | At least one I → II | At least two I → II |
| Doctrine contribution | None expected | None required | At least one curriculum improvement | At least one Gold Standard authored or doctrine amendment driven |
| Customer NPS bar | ≥ 30 | ≥ 50 | ≥ 60 | ≥ 70 |
| NRR bar (owned portfolio) | ≥ 95% | ≥ 105% | ≥ 115% | ≥ 125% |
| GRR bar (owned portfolio) | ≥ 85% | ≥ 90% | ≥ 92% | ≥ 95% |
| Customer CEO interaction | Observed/supervised | Direct, all owned | Strategic + multi-customer | Segment-level + industry-facing |
| Renewal cadence | Annual | Annual | Biennial | Biennial |

Every promotion between tiers requires three signals to align:

1. **The numbers** — scorecard bars hit.
2. **The artifacts** — real recorded work, real implementation case studies (per Section 7 of this framework).
3. **The judgment call** — the candidate's manager AND one external reviewer both believe they're ready.

Don't promote on time-in-seat. Don't promote because someone is bored. Don't promote to retain.

## 2. Operator I — The Apprentice Practitioner

### What Operator I signifies

> *"I can execute the Academy method on small customer accounts, under weekly supervision, with documented evidence of at least one closed Loop."*

The Operator I credential is the first real Academy promise. It says the holder is past Apprentice — they can do useful customer-facing work without a senior on every call. They are not yet trusted with mid-tier accounts or solo QBR delivery, but they can run the Weekly Vital Read, draft Customer Economics analyses, and close simple Loops.

### Required competencies

From the [Competency Model](./00-academy-overview/02-competency-model.md) (7 pillars, 34 competencies, 0–3 scale):

| Pillar | Bar |
|---|---|
| 01 SaaS Foundations | All 5 competencies at level 1+, at least 2 at level 2 |
| 02 Cleaning Operations | All 5 competencies at level 1+, at least 2.1 (Service Models) and 2.2 (Labor Economics) at level 2 |
| 03 PioneerOps Platform Mastery | All 6 competencies at level 2+ (THE platform pillar — minimum bar is higher here) |
| 04 AI Operator | 4.1 (Prompt Patterns), 4.5 (AI Judgment) at level 1+; rest may be 0 |
| 05 Customer Success | 5.1 (Discovery), 5.2 (Onboarding) at level 1+; 5.3 (QBR) NOT required |
| 06 Revenue Operations | 6.1 (Pipeline Hygiene) at level 1; rest may be 0 |
| 07 Executive Advisor | 7.1 (CEO Conversation) at level 1+; rest may be 0 |

**Crystal-clear bar:** at least 15 of 34 competencies at level 2 or higher, NO competencies at level 0.

### Required lessons (must be completed AND quizzed)

The four foundation lessons:

| Lesson | Status |
|---|---|
| [02.2 Revenue Per Labor Hour](./02-cleaning-operations/02-revenue-per-labor-hour.md) | Required · live |
| [02.3 Customer Economics](./02-cleaning-operations/03-customer-economics.md) | Required · live |
| [03.1 Mission Control Diagnostics](./03-pioneerops-platform-mastery/01-mission-control-diagnostics.md) | Required · live |
| [05.3 QBR Delivery](./05-customer-success/03-qbr-delivery.md) | Required · read for context only; full QBR delivery NOT required at this tier |

Quiz scores: ≥ 7/10 on at least three of the four lessons' quizzes.

### Required practical demonstrations

| Demonstration | Bar |
|---|---|
| Weekly Vital Read | Delivered solo on at least 2 owned accounts for 4 consecutive weeks (8 reads total, recorded) |
| Loop entries | At least 3 created with named verification dates; at least 1 fully closed (verification logged) |
| Customer Economics analysis | At least 1 produced on a real owned account, with arithmetic visible (Pillar 7) |
| Operational Feed review | Has reviewed at least 20 distinct operational events and categorized them |
| Pillar 8 verification practice | Has performed at least 1 record-verification pause before recommending action |

The "1 fully closed Loop" requirement is the doctrine pivot. A candidate with 100 Loop entries and zero closed Loops fails. A candidate with 3 Loop entries — one closed honestly with verified negative outcome, two still open with future dates — passes.

### Required portfolio evidence

Submitted as a single package for review:

| Artifact | Description |
|---|---|
| Apprentice quiz scores | All four foundation lesson quizzes |
| Weekly Vital Read recordings | 8 total (2 accounts × 4 weeks); reviewer-watchable |
| Loop entry log | All entries created; honest record of which closed, which failed, which open |
| Customer Economics analysis | 1 complete analysis with reviewer-defensible math |
| Self-rating | Across all 34 competencies; candidate's honest assessment |
| Trainer's confirmation | One paragraph from the trainer who supervised |
| The Pioneer Test reflection | One paragraph: "Why I think this work passes the Pioneer Test." |

### Required business outcomes

At least **one verified positive business outcome** on an owned account, attributable to the candidate's work, with documented baseline → action → verification.

Examples that qualify:

- Customer's RPLH on one account moved from $X to $X+5 after operator-led intervention
- Customer's inspection coverage on a building moved from 70% to 90%+ after operator-led intervention
- One churn-risk signal caught early and resolved (operator-attributable)

Examples that do NOT qualify:

- "Customer engagement improved" (not measurable)
- "Customer says they're happier" (not the right metric — see Pillar 7)
- "Customer used Mission Control more often" (use is not outcome)
- Aggregate company-level metrics that the candidate didn't directly influence

### Required role-play assessments

Scenario A from at least 2 of the 4 foundation lesson role-plays, passed at certification grade per the rubric in each lesson:

| Lesson | Scenario A required? |
|---|---|
| 02.2 RPLH (not a role-play lesson — no scenario) | n/a |
| 02.3 Customer Economics — "Revenue is up. We're crushing it." | At least Scenario A pass required if chosen |
| 05.3 QBR Delivery — "The CEO who came to leave" | Pass NOT required at Operator I; observation-only acceptable |
| 03.1 MC Diagnostics — "First Vital Read with skeptical CEO" | At least Scenario A pass required if chosen |

**Minimum pass: 2 of the available 3 role-play Scenario A assessments**. The candidate may choose which 2.

### Required executive communication standards

| Standard | Bar at Operator I |
|---|---|
| 90-second compression | Can deliver the 90-second version of ONE topic at certification grade (Customer Economics OR Vital Read OR Loop closure) |
| Pioneer Test reflex | Can self-identify lessons that fail the Pioneer Test in a peer's draft work |
| Banned vagueness | Can identify the vague phrases from Constitution Section "Language Precision" in their own writing and re-write them |
| Written follow-up doc | Can produce a 24-hour follow-up doc for a Vital Read meeting their account ran |
| Customer CEO solo conversation | NOT required at this tier; observed conversation is acceptable |

### Required Financial Pulse capabilities

| Capability | Bar at Operator I |
|---|---|
| Can name all six vitals | Yes — perfect recall |
| Can read each vital | At least 4 of 6: Labor Efficiency, Margin Integrity, Operational Exposure, and one of the others |
| Knows threshold defaults | At least Margin Integrity + Operational Exposure (the two most critical for cleaning operations) |
| Pattern Library recognition | Can recognize at least 5 of the 14 patterns when shown the trigger condition |
| Has driven a Vital Read with a customer | Yes — at least 2 customers, weekly, 4 weeks |
| Has executed at least one Pattern Library response | Yes — at least 1 (which pattern is the candidate's choice) |

### Cannot do (yet)

- Run QBRs for customer CEOs (deferred to Operator II)
- Make pricing recommendations to CEOs
- Negotiate contract changes
- Lead a Pillar 8 Discontinuity diagnosis solo
- Mentor other Operators (informal coaching is fine)
- Carry strategic accounts

### Compensation signal

Standard starting role salary. Variable compensation opens — small portion tied to retention of owned accounts.

### Renewal

Annual. Renewal evidence: at least 2 closed Loops per year on owned accounts; no demoted competencies; Pioneer Test still passable on recent work.

## 3. Operator II — The Independent Practitioner

### What Operator II signifies

> *"I can run the full Loop independently across a portfolio of mid-tier accounts. I deliver QBRs to customer CEOs that produce executive decisions. I am driving at least one customer toward self-sustaining Vital Reads. My evidence record contains both wins and named honest failures."*

This is the **standard graduate operator** the Academy aims to produce. Operator II is what "passes basic training" looks like in a way the market will respect.

### Required competencies

| Pillar | Bar |
|---|---|
| 01 SaaS Foundations | All 5 at level 2+, at least 2 at level 3 |
| 02 Cleaning Operations | All 5 at level 2+, at least 2.2 (Labor Economics) at level 3 |
| 03 PioneerOps Platform Mastery | All 6 at level 2+, at least 03.5 (Mission Control) at level 3 |
| 04 AI Operator | All 5 at level 2+ (the AI division of labor is now non-optional) |
| 05 Customer Success | All 5 at level 2+, at least 5.3 (QBR) at level 3 |
| 06 Revenue Operations | All 4 at level 2+ |
| 07 Executive Advisor | 7.1 (CEO Conversation) at level 3, others at level 2+ |

**Crystal-clear bar:** all 34 competencies at level 2 or higher, at least 8 at level 3, including 02.2 (Labor Economics), 03.5 (Mission Control), 04.5 (AI Judgment), 05.3 (QBR Delivery), and 07.1 (CEO Conversation).

### Required lessons

| Lesson | Status |
|---|---|
| All four foundation lessons | Required · already complete from Operator I |
| All advanced lessons that exist in the curriculum at certification time | Required · the bar moves as the Academy grows |

At time of this framework's authoring (2026-06-11), the advanced lessons are not yet published. As Pillar 04 (AI Operator), 05 (Customer Success), 06 (Revenue Ops), and 07 (Executive Advisor) ship Gold Standard or Academy Standard lessons, those become required for Operator II at the time of their certification.

**Quiz bar:** ≥ 8/10 on at least 4 of the lesson quizzes; ≥ 9/10 on the QBR Delivery quiz (Q10 compression rubric pass required).

### Required practical demonstrations

| Demonstration | Bar |
|---|---|
| QBR delivery | At least 8 delivered to customer CEOs, with rubric pass on at least 6 (trainer-scored) |

| Self-Sustaining Loop verified | At least 1 customer running their own Vital Read solo at the moment of certification, with documented examples of CEO catching deviations without operator on the call |
| Loop entries | At least 12 created across the certification period; at least 8 closed (verification logged, including failures) |
| Churn-risk save | At least 1 documented (account flagged at risk, intervention executed, renewal at full value or expanded) |
| Expansion closed | At least 1 expansion deal (multi-location, premium tier, or add-on module) — operator-attributable |
| Pillar 8 Discontinuity handled | At least 1 documented case of pausing before action because the underlying record was suspect |

### Required portfolio evidence

| Executive briefing | 1 delivered to internal panel; 15 minutes; recorded |
| Difficult conversation | 1 recorded or transcribed (anonymized) |
| Customer health model | 1 in active weekly use |
| Self-Sustaining Loop evidence | Documented examples of a customer running reads without operator on the call |
| Self-rating | Across all 34 competencies |
| Trainer + reviewer confirmations | Both required |

### Required business outcomes

Verified on the candidate's owned account portfolio:

| GRR (trailing 12) | ≥ 90% |

## 6. Cross-Tier Requirements

Some standards apply to all four tiers regardless of level.

### Every Operator at every tier must

1. **Hold the Pioneer Test as a reflex.** Can apply it to own work without being asked.
2. **Carry the Economic Reality chain as a habit of mind.** No analysis presented without the chain completed.
3. **Respect Pillar 8.** No claim presented without an auditable record beneath it.
4. **Use AI with judgment.** Neither refusing AI (Pillar 5 violation) nor deferring to it (Pillar 5 violation).
5. **Speak with language precision.** No banned vagueness; sourced numbers; labeled certainty.
6. **Honor the Operator Mindset standards** (Thesis Section 13): truth over comfort, ownership of outcomes, calm in ambiguity, economic conscience, respect for the field, skepticism of own dashboards, bias toward simplification, long loops held open.

### Every Operator at every tier must NOT

1. Sign off on claims they cannot defend in real time
2. Recommend action without the Pillar 7 chain completed
3. Promise customers outcomes the operator cannot verify
4. Skip role-play preparation under time pressure
5. Mark a Loop closed without documented verification (including verified failure)
6. Use "profit" when the doctrine says "Customer Economics"
7. Use "dashboard" when the doctrine says "diagnostic instrument"
8. Curate their own evidence record (only-wins disqualifies at all tiers)

## 7. The Certification Process

How a certification happens from start to finish.

### Step 1 — Candidate Declares Intent

The candidate, with their direct manager, submits a formal intent-to-certify notification to the Academy lead. The notification includes:

- Current tier (or Apprentice if no prior tier)
- Target tier
- Estimated date of certification readiness
- A self-assessment against the target tier's bars (every section above)

If the self-assessment shows obvious gaps, the Academy lead may decline to schedule certification until the gaps are addressed. This is not punitive; it saves everyone's time.

### Step 2 — The Evidence Pack

The candidate submits the evidence package required for their target tier (Sections 2–5 above) as a single bundle in a single shared folder.

The pack must include:

- All required artifacts (per the tier's section)
- A signed certification request from the candidate's direct manager
- An independent reviewer recommendation from at least one Operator at or above the target tier
- The candidate's self-assessment

### Step 3 — Reviewer Review

Two reviewers, independently, evaluate the evidence pack:

- **Primary reviewer** — the candidate's trainer or direct manager
- **Secondary reviewer** — a Senior Operator (at or above target tier) not directly involved with the candidate's day-to-day work

Each reviewer:

- Rates each requirement section (per the tier's bar) on a 0–4 scale
- Provides a written defense of each rating
- Flags any of the 8 Cross-Tier "Must Not" violations
- Applies the **Pioneer Test** (Constitution v1.1) to the entire pack

Reviewers must be willing to defend their ratings against the other reviewer. Disagreements escalate to the Academy lead.

### Step 4 — Decision

The Academy lead reviews the two reviewer ratings + the Pioneer Test outcomes + the candidate's evidence pack.

Three possible decisions:

| Decision | When applies |
|---|---|
| **Pass** | All requirement sections ≥ 2 from both reviewers AND Pioneer Test pass AND no "Must Not" violations AND the qualitative judgment of the Academy lead aligns |
| **Conditional pass** | Both reviewers see at least 6 of 8 requirement sections at 2+; one specific gap to close in 30 days; Pioneer Test pass; no "Must Not" violations |
| **No pass — gap-driven** | 2+ requirement sections below 2 OR Pioneer Test fail. 30-day remediation plan with explicit bars to clear before re-test. |
| **No pass — disqualifying** | Any "Must Not" violation OR an evidence record containing only successes (curation flag). 60–90 day remediation OR role-fit conversation. |

### Step 5 — Decision Delivery

For each decision, the Academy lead delivers:

- Written feedback (1 page minimum) — what passed, what didn't, what's next
- The reviewer ratings (both)
- The Pioneer Test outcome
- The specific bars to clear if not a full pass
- The next certification window date if applicable

The candidate has 14 days to request a single re-review by an additional senior reviewer if they believe the decision was incorrect. Re-reviews do not change initial decisions in the candidate's favor without compelling new evidence.

### Step 6 — Recognition + Compensation

For a pass:

- Title change effective
- Compensation adjustment per band
- Public acknowledgment in Academy + customer roster
- Lineage entry: who they mentored, who they were mentored by

### Step 7 — Renewal Schedule Begins

The renewal calendar starts on certification date. Renewal evidence is captured continuously through normal operations and submitted on schedule per the tier's renewal requirements.

## 7.5 Bootstrap Phase Reviewer Provisions

> **Added in Framework v1.1.** Active. Sunset condition specified below.

### The problem this section addresses

Section 7 Step 3 requires the **Secondary Reviewer** be a "Senior Operator (at or above target tier) not directly involved with the candidate's day-to-day work."

At Framework v1 publication, **no Operators have been certified at any tier**. The requirement is therefore structurally unsatisfiable for the first certifications. Without explicit provision, the framework cannot run a first cycle.

This section creates a time-bounded exception: a **Bootstrap Reviewer** role that meets the framework's intent (independent, capable, credible review) without requiring a credential that does not yet exist.

### Bootstrap Reviewer eligibility

A Bootstrap Reviewer is an individual who:

1. Has demonstrated **equivalent capability** through documented field practice for the target tier (specifics below)
2. Has read all senior governance docs end-to-end ([Thesis](./ACADEMY-THESIS.md), [Constitution v1.1](./CONSTITUTION.md), this Framework, [Academy Gold Standard](./academy-gold-standard.md), [Financial Pulse Operating System](../financial-pulse-framework/01-financial-pulse-operating-system.md))
3. Has been independently confirmed by the Academy lead as capable of grading at the target tier
4. Is **not** the candidate's direct manager AND **not** their day-to-day customer-success counterpart on the same account

### Equivalent capability by target tier

For each target tier, a Bootstrap Reviewer must demonstrate the equivalent-capability criteria below. The Academy lead is the final arbiter of whether a proposed Bootstrap Reviewer meets the bar.

| Target tier | Bootstrap Reviewer must demonstrate |
|---|---|
| **Operator I** | At least 12 months of documented customer-facing work in commercial cleaning, field services, or B2B SaaS customer success; familiarity with the foundation lessons' content; can pass the foundation lesson quizzes ≥ 7/10 |
| **Operator II** | At least 24 months of documented customer-success leadership; at least 12 QBR-equivalent meetings delivered; demonstrated portfolio NRR > 100% (or equivalent retention metric); capability to deliver any foundation-lesson role-play scenario as the customer-CEO actor |
| **Operator III** | At least 36 months of executive-level customer leadership; documented mentorship of others through their development; demonstrated strategic-account ownership |
| **Executive Advisor** | At least 60 months of customer-success / operational leadership; multiple direct reports who themselves meet the equivalent-capability bar; published or otherwise documented thought leadership in the operational space |

**Note:** Equivalent capability is judged on demonstrated practice, not credentials. A 24-month senior CSM at another company with the right outcome record qualifies; a 36-month CSM whose portfolio didn't retain does not.

### Bootstrap Phase scope

| Phase boundary | Specification |
|---|---|
| Begins | At publication of Framework v1.1 (2026-06-11) |
| Active | For each tier independently, until 3 Operators are certified at or above that tier under the standard framework |
| Ends per tier | When the Academy lead announces "Bootstrap sunset for tier N" via a formal amendment to this section |

Tier sunsets are tier-specific. Operator I bootstrap may end years before Executive Advisor bootstrap.

### Bootstrap-specific safeguards

During Bootstrap Phase, every certification is subject to additional protections that compensate for the absence of certified peers as reviewers:

#### Safeguard 1 — Triple-Reviewer Minimum

In addition to Primary Reviewer + Bootstrap Secondary Reviewer, the Academy lead serves as a **third reviewer** (rather than only the standard two-reviewer + lead-decision model).

All three reviewers complete the [reviewer-evaluation template](./templates/certification-reviewer-evaluation.md) independently. The Academy lead's role expands from "decision based on two reviewers" to "third reviewer + decision."

The reviewer-evaluation form is filled out by the Academy lead BEFORE seeing the other two reviewers' submissions, to preserve independence.

#### Safeguard 2 — Stricter Evidence Requirements

Bootstrap-period candidates must produce evidence packs that **exceed** the standard Section 2–5 bars by approximately 20% in artifact volume.

- Operator I (standard 8 Vital Reads) → Bootstrap requires 10
- Operator II (standard 8 QBRs) → Bootstrap requires 10
- Operator II (standard 12 Loop entries) → Bootstrap requires 15
- All tier-specific bars proportionally elevated

The Academy lead documents the elevated targets at certification scheduling time.

#### Safeguard 3 — Public Review

Bootstrap-phase decisions are published in the [Certification Registry](./certification-registry.md) **with full reviewer rating context** — not just pass/fail. Specifically:

- Each reviewer's section-by-section ratings (anonymized to position, e.g., "Primary," "Bootstrap Secondary," "Academy Lead")
- The Pioneer Test outcome
- The specific safeguards applied
- The decision rationale paragraph

This is more transparency than the standard framework requires. It compensates for the unavailability of standard peer review.

#### Safeguard 4 — Sunset Re-Review

**Every Bootstrap-Phase certification must be re-reviewed against the standard framework once Bootstrap Phase ends for that tier.**

The re-review:

- Is conducted by two certified Senior Operators (now available)
- Uses the candidate's then-current evidence (12–24 months of accumulated practice since original certification)
- Either **confirms** the original certification, OR **downgrades** per [Section 9 (Demotion Process)](#9-demotion-process)

Bootstrap certifications are valid until the re-review. They are not provisional. But the re-review IS a structural commitment to ensuring the Bootstrap exception did not under-bar anyone.

### Bootstrap Reviewer roster

The Academy lead maintains a list of approved Bootstrap Reviewers for each tier. The roster is internal (not in the public registry).

Roster entries include:

- Reviewer name
- Approved for tier(s)
- Date approved
- Equivalent capability evidence (link or summary)
- Conflict-of-interest declarations (which Pioneer staff they cannot review for)

A Bootstrap Reviewer's status is revoked if any of the eligibility criteria lapse, or if their reviews show systematic over- or under-rating during cohort calibration.

### When this section is amended

Amendments to this section follow the standard framework amendment process ([Section 16](#16-amendment-process-for-this-framework)). The most likely amendments:

- Sunsets per tier (when 3 certified Operators exist at or above)
- Refinements to equivalent-capability bars based on field experience
- Removal of the entire section once all tiers have sunset

When the last tier sunsets, this section moves to historical with a note in the Amendment Log.

## 8. Renewal Process

Certifications expire. Renewal is evidence-based.

### When renewal is due

- Operator I: annually
- Operator II: annually
- Operator III: biennially
- Executive Advisor: biennially

### What renewal requires

For every tier:

| Requirement | Description |
|---|---|
| Continued business outcomes | Tier-level NRR/GRR/NPS bars maintained over the renewal period |
| Continued Loop closure | At least 4 closed Loops per year on owned accounts (more for higher tiers) |
| No demoted competencies | Self-assessment + reviewer assessment confirms competencies have not regressed |
| Currency with doctrine | Has read and can articulate any Constitution amendments since prior renewal |
| Recent work portfolio | Sample artifacts (QBRs, follow-up docs, etc.) from the renewal period |

### Renewal decisions

- **Renew** — all requirements met. Cert continues for another period.
- **Renew with caution** — soft signal of regression in one or two areas. Cert continues with documented improvement plan.
- **Demote** — significant regression. See Section 9.
- **Decertify** — disqualifying behavior (record curation, dishonest evidence). Cert revoked.

### Renewal cycle exceptions

A candidate engaged in a major life event (parental leave, medical leave, etc.) may pause the renewal cycle for up to 12 months with written notification. The cycle resumes when the candidate returns to active work.

## 9. Demotion Process

Certifications can be demoted or revoked. The framework protects the credential, not the holder.

### Reasons for demotion

1. **Regression of competencies.** Self-assessment + reviewer assessment shows multiple competencies dropped a level since last renewal.
2. **Failed renewal evidence.** Business outcomes regressed below tier bar over the full renewal period.
3. **Evidence record curation.** Recent record shows only successes; honest failures have stopped being documented.
4. **Persistent Pioneer Test failures.** Work submitted in the renewal period repeatedly fails the Pioneer Test on Academy review.
5. **Disqualifying behavior.** Dishonest evidence, plagiarism of others' work, persistent Pillar 8 violations.

### Demotion decisions

| Decision | When applies |
|---|---|
| **Demote one tier** | Reasons 1, 2, or 4 (significant but recoverable) |
| **Demote two tiers** | Reason 3 (record curation) — implicit indicator of broader integrity issue |
| **Revoke certification entirely** | Reason 5 (disqualifying behavior) |

### Demotion process

1. Academy lead notifies the Operator in writing with specific evidence
2. The Operator has 30 days to respond and present counter-evidence
3. A second senior reviewer reviews independently
4. Decision delivered with written rationale
5. If demoted, the Operator may re-attempt their prior tier certification at the next regular cycle

Demotion is rare. Demotion is also non-negotiable when warranted.

## 10. Tier Promotion Process

Promotion from one tier to the next.

### Promotion eligibility

A candidate is eligible for promotion when:

- Their current tier has been held for the minimum required time (per the Section 1 table)
- Their evidence record demonstrates capability at the target tier
- Their current-tier scorecard bars are not just met but exceeded

### Promotion process

1. The current-tier renewal cycle and promotion cycle do not overlap. A candidate is not renewed and promoted simultaneously.
2. Promotion candidate submits an evidence pack per Section 7.
3. Reviewers grade per Section 7.
4. Academy lead decides per Section 7.

### When promotion is denied

- The candidate retains their current tier
- They may re-attempt promotion at the next certification cycle (typically 6 months)
- Repeated denials (3+) trigger a role-fit conversation

## 11. The Apprentice Pre-Certification

Before Operator I, a candidate is an **Apprentice**.

The Apprentice tier is NOT a certification. It is a status — held while the candidate is in initial training. Apprentices:

- Do not own customer accounts
- Pair with Senior Operators on customer calls
- Complete the four foundation lesson quizzes
- Build initial portfolio evidence
- Practice role-play scenarios under coaching

Apprentices automatically promote to Operator I when they meet the Operator I bar. There is no separate "Apprentice certification."

This formalizes the existing Apprentice tier from the prior Certification Ladder doc.

## 12. The Certification Identifier System

The framework introduces a single string identifier for any certified Operator:

```
POPS-OP-{Tier}-{YYYY}-{LineageID}
```

Where:
- `POPS-OP` = PioneerOps Operator
- `Tier` = I / II / III / EA
- `YYYY` = year of original certification
- `LineageID` = unique 4-digit ID, sequential

Example: `POPS-OP-II-2026-0142` = the 142nd Operator II ever certified, certified in 2026.

The Lineage ID is permanent — it travels with the Operator across tiers, across renewals, across employers. An Operator who joins another company can still be verified against the Academy registry.

Why this matters: as the credential gains market value (per the Thesis Vision), the verification mechanism must already exist. Building it into the framework day one is cheaper than retrofitting later.

## 13. What This Framework Is NOT

### NOT a hiring framework

This framework does not specify who Pioneer hires. It specifies how candidates already hired get credentialed.

### NOT an employment guarantee

Holding a certification does not guarantee employment at Pioneer or anywhere else. Certifications signal capability; employment is a business decision.

### NOT a Pioneer-specific lock-in

A certified Operator who leaves Pioneer for another company keeps their certification (and renewal obligations). The credential is portable.

### NOT a substitute for performance management

Operators with certifications still report to managers, get performance reviews, and can be performance-managed out. Certifications and employment are separate systems.

### NOT a closed system

External candidates (consultants, contractors, customers' own teams) may eventually be eligible for certification through an open path. That path is out of scope for v1 but is named here so the framework's eventual broadening is anticipated.

## 15. Open Questions

Questions whose answers are not yet locked, but will need to be before scaling certifications:

1. **External certification eligibility.** When (if ever) does this framework open to non-Pioneer-employed candidates? Possibly v2.
2. **Industry-recognition strategy.** What does it take for the credential to be recognized at the level of CPA, PMP, Six Sigma? Likely years of work.
3. **Disqualifying behavior catalog.** Section 9 mentions "disqualifying behavior" — needs an explicit, named catalog beyond the examples (similar to Constitution's "Banned vagueness" table).
4. **Cross-industry certifications.** The Thesis says doctrine is industry-agnostic; certifications might one day differentiate (e.g., Operator II — Cleaning Operations; Operator II — Telecom Construction). Out of v1 scope.
5. **AI certification depth.** Pillar 5 (AI Operator) competencies are tier-graduated. As AI capability evolves, does the bar move within tier (without changing tier)? Probably yes — amend annually.
6. **Operator I/II/III tier names internationally.** "Operator" translates differently in different markets. Out of scope; flagged.

These questions are not blockers — v1 can ship with defaults and amend. But each should have an answer before mature operation.

## 16. Amendment Process for This Framework

Same standard as the Constitution and Thesis:

1. **Evidence first.** Amendments begin with verified field evidence — a certification produced a wrong result, a bar proved too high or too low, a process step proved unworkable.
2. **Diagnosed, not patched.** Failure traced to the specific principle and its application boundary.
3. **Amended visibly.** Written, dated, reasoned, propagated to all reviewers and candidates.
4. **Verified after.** Amendments carry a prediction (what will change) and a review date.

**Amendment authority:** Academy lead, with consultation from the Executive Advisor cohort (if one exists). Where consultation produces deadlock, the Thesis governs.
