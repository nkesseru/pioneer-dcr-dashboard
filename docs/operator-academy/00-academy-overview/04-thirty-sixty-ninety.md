# 04 — 30/60/90 Day Path

The week-by-week schedule for the first 90 days. This is a starting template — adjust for the candidate, the customer load, and the curriculum's current maturity.

## Operating assumptions

- **5 work days per week.** ~40 hours. No heroic schedules during onboarding; tired operators learn nothing.
- **Trainer cadence:** Monday/Wednesday/Friday touchpoints (see [05 — Coaching Rhythm](./05-coaching-rhythm.md)).
- **Pairing partner:** A Senior Operator or, for the first cohort, Nick directly.
- **Customer exposure starts week 2.** Don't wait — operators learn from real conversations.

---

## Week 1 — Orientation

### Goals
- Understand what Pioneer is, what we sell, and to whom
- Tour every PioneerOps screen at least once
- Meet every internal stakeholder
- Sign up for AI tooling (Claude, Cursor, whatever's current)

### Schedule

| Day | Focus | Output |
|---|---|---|
| Mon | Pioneer founder story + customer roster intro | Notes on each customer's category, size, age |
| Tue | PioneerOps platform tour (every screen) | Annotated screenshot deck |
| Wed | Cleaning industry primer + read 5 industry articles | One-page industry summary |
| Thu | AI tool setup + first prompt-pattern session | Personal prompt library doc started |
| Fri | Week 1 debrief + scorecard review | Honest self-rating across 34 competencies |

### Reading list (assigned end of week 0)

- The cleaning industry overview (BSCAI / ISSA primers)
- "The Founder's Mentality" — Chris Zook (10 pages, not the whole book)
- One QBR transcript from a Pioneer customer (find one in the Comms threads)
- The PioneerOps Mission Control product spec docs

### End of week 1 — Apprentice quiz, version 1

## Week 2 — Cleaning Reality

The point of week 2 is to make sure the operator never forgets that our software runs a business where someone gets up at 3 AM to clean a building.

### Goals
- Complete one full overnight cleaning shift (mandatory)
- Shadow one field tech for a partial day shift
- Sit in on one customer call (kickoff, QBR, or escalation)
- Begin Pillar 02 (Cleaning Operations) coursework

### Schedule

| Day | Focus | Output |
|---|---|---|
| Mon | Cleaning ops Pillar 02 — lessons 1–3 | Workbook entries |
| Tue | **OVERNIGHT SHIFT** at a Pioneer customer (10pm–4am typical) | Written reflection: what surprised you |
| Wed | Recover. Cleaning ops Pillar 02 — lessons 4–5 | Workbook entries |
| Thu | Field tech ride-along (day porter or restock route) | Photos + observations |
| Fri | Customer call shadow + Pillar 02 quiz | Quiz score; debrief |

### Reflection prompts after the shift

- What was harder than you expected?
- What was easier?
- What would make the night-shift tech's job 10% better?
- How does what you saw show up (or not) in PioneerOps?

- Pillar 04 (AI Operator) competencies 04.1, 04.5 at level 1
- Apprentice certification pass
- First account assignment (small, supervised)

### Schedule

| Day | Focus | Output |
|---|---|---|
| Mon | Communication threads + SMS transport | Run a thread end to end |
| Tue | Mission Control + financial layers | Walk through Customer Economics card with Senior |
| Wed | Admin tools, roles, integrations | Configure a test customer |
| Thu | AI Operator intro (Pillar 04) | First analytical artifact built with Claude |
| Fri | **Apprentice Certification** | Pass/fail. If pass → Operator I status. |

### Apprentice certification checklist

- [ ] All Pillar 03 competencies at level 1+
- [ ] Apprentice quiz score ≥ 80%
- [ ] Overnight shift completed + reflection submitted
- [ ] 5 shadowed customer calls logged
- [ ] Manager rating ≥ 3/5 on coachability, curiosity, customer empathy
- [ ] Self-rating across 34 competencies (no scoring; baseline only)

### Account assignment at end of week 4

If certified Operator I: receive 1–2 small accounts to own. Always with a Senior co-pilot for the first 30 days of ownership.

### Week 12 — Executive Briefing + Certification

| Day | Focus | Output |
|---|---|---|
| Mon | Executive briefing prep (Pillar 07) | Deck drafted |
| Tue | Deliver executive briefing to internal panel | Recorded |
| Wed | Pillar 07.4 — Pushback workshop | Roleplay |
| Thu | Operator II competency review | All 34 rated |
| Fri | **Operator II Certification** | Pass/fail decision. If pass → graduated operator. |

### Operator II certification checklist

- [ ] All 34 competencies at level 2+
- [ ] At least 5 competencies at level 3
- [ ] 8+ QBRs delivered with manager score ≥ 4/5
- [ ] Implementation case study submitted + accepted
- [ ] Executive briefing delivered to internal panel (recorded)
- [ ] At least one expansion deal closed
- [ ] At least one churn-risk save documented
- [ ] Customer NPS from owned accounts ≥ 50

If pass: full Operator II status. Compensation adjusted. Account portfolio expanded to 8–12 mid-tier customers.

If fail: structured remediation plan, extend 30 days, re-test. Two failures = role-fit conversation.

---

## What happens after day 90

Days 91+: Operator II owns their portfolio. Trainer cadence drops to biweekly 1:1. New competencies developed organically through real customer work. Senior Operator track opens at month 10.

## A note on pace

Some operators graduate in 60 days. Some take 120. The bars are the bars — don't compress to hit a calendar, don't pad to keep someone comfortable. Most importantly: don't graduate someone who isn't ready, no matter how much you like them.
