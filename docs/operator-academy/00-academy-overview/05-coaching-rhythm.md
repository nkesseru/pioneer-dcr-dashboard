# 05 — Weekly Coaching Rhythm

Three touchpoints per week. Each has a specific purpose. Each is short. Each is non-negotiable.

## The three meetings

| Meeting | When | Duration | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Pipeline Review** | Monday morning | 60 min | What's happening this week? |
| **Skill Drill** | Wednesday | 45 min | One competency, deliberate practice |
| **Weekly Debrief** | Friday afternoon | 30 min | What worked, what didn't, scorecard update |

Total time: 2 hours 15 minutes per week. That's the floor for a trainer's investment. If your trainer can't commit this, the operator will plateau.

---

## Meeting 1 — Monday Pipeline Review

**Time:** Monday 9:00–10:00am (or first thing for whatever shift the operator works).
**Attendees:** Operator + trainer.
**Format:** Operator drives. Trainer asks questions.

### Standing agenda

1. **Account-by-account walk** (30 min)
   - For each owned account: health score, last touchpoint, next planned touchpoint, top risk, top opportunity
   - Identify the 1–2 accounts that need extra focus this week
2. **Calendar review** (10 min)
   - Calls this week
   - Deliverables this week (drafts due, decks due, internal asks)
3. **Trainer feedback** (10 min)
   - On last week's customer interactions (recordings or notes)
   - One thing to keep doing, one thing to change
4. **Commitments** (10 min)
   - What will the operator complete by Friday?
   - Written down. Reviewed Friday.

### What good looks like

The operator walks into Monday already knowing every account's status. They've reviewed PioneerOps health scores, customer comms, and any internal escalations before the meeting starts. The trainer adds context, not data.

### What broken looks like

The operator is hearing about their own accounts from the trainer. The trainer is taking notes for the operator. This means the operator hasn't done the prep, or the trainer is doing the operator's job. Either way, name it and fix it.

## Meeting 2 — Wednesday Skill Drill

**Time:** Wednesday afternoon (energy is right for practice work).
**Attendees:** Operator + trainer (and sometimes another operator).
**Format:** One competency. Deliberate practice. Recorded.

### Why this is the most important meeting

Operators learn from customer calls — but customer calls are not safe practice environments. Skill drills are. The Wednesday drill is where you make mistakes on purpose so you don't make them in front of a real customer.

### Format

1. **Pick the competency** (3 min)
   - Trainer chooses based on the operator's current gap
   - Rotate through the 34 competencies; never the same one two weeks in a row
2. **Set up the scenario** (5 min)
   - Use a real anonymized customer situation
   - Trainer plays the customer; operator plays themselves
3. **Run the drill** (15 min)
   - Recorded
4. **Stop, rewind, replay** (15 min)
   - Watch the recording together
   - Trainer asks: "what would you do differently?"
   - Re-run the drill with the adjustment
5. **Commit** (7 min)
   - One specific change the operator will apply in real calls this week
   - Written down. Reviewed Friday.

### Drill sources

- `templates/roleplay-scenarios.md` — the catalog of pre-built scenarios
- Recent real customer situations (anonymized) — best source
- "What if" variations on real situations — "what if the customer had pushed back here?"

### A rotation suggestion

| Week | Competency focus |
|---|---|
| 1 | 05.1 Discovery |
| 2 | 05.3 QBR delivery |
| 3 | 05.5 Difficult conversations |
| 4 | 07.1 CEO-level conversation |
| 5 | 06.3 Expansion plays |
| 6 | 07.4 Pushback & disagreement |
| 7 | 02.2 Labor economics conversation |
| 8 | 03.5 Mission Control walkthrough |
| ... | (rotate; weight toward operator's weak areas) |

## Meeting 3 — Friday Debrief

**Time:** Friday 3:00–3:30pm. End the week with reflection.
**Attendees:** Operator + trainer.
**Format:** Two-direction feedback. Scorecard update.

### Standing agenda

1. **Did Monday's commitments land?** (5 min)
   - Yes/no on each item
   - If no — why?
2. **Customer outcomes this week** (10 min)
   - Health score deltas
   - Any churn signal? Any expansion signal?
   - One customer-specific lesson learned
3. **Trainer → Operator feedback** (5 min)
   - One thing that worked
   - One thing to change next week
4. **Operator → Trainer feedback** (5 min)
   - What did the operator need that they didn't get?
   - What should the curriculum do differently?
5. **Scorecard update** (5 min)
   - See `templates/scorecard.md`
   - Quantitative numbers in
   - Qualitative ratings discussed

### Why two-direction feedback matters

The trainer is also learning. The Academy is a v1 product; every week of feedback from the operator improves it for the next cohort. If the operator never tells the trainer they're confused, the curriculum stays bad.

## Monthly: Customer Ride-Along

Once per month (more in the first 90 days), the operator spends a day in a customer's actual operation. Not on a call — physically with them. Sit in their dispatch office. Walk a route. Watch their billing person process invoices. Ask why they do things the way they do.

This is the single highest-leverage learning activity in the Academy. It's also the easiest to skip. Don't skip it.

### Format

- Schedule with a friendly customer 2 weeks ahead
- Operator goes solo (or with another operator)
- 6–8 hour visit
- Written debrief: 3 things you learned, 1 thing PioneerOps should do differently
- Share the debrief with the team

---

## Quarterly: QBR Delivery Practice

Once per quarter, the operator delivers a "mock QBR" to an internal panel (trainer + 1–2 other operators + Nick if available).

### Format

- Real customer data (one of the operator's accounts)
- Real 30-minute QBR deck
- Real CEO-level audience pretending to be the customer's CEO
- 15 min Q&A with hostile-but-fair questions
- Score against the QBR rubric (in `templates/certification-rubric.md`)

This is the practice that lets operators graduate from competent QBR delivery to confident QBR delivery.

---

## Annually: 360 Review

Once per year, gather feedback from:

- Their trainer
- 3 customers they own
- 2 internal stakeholders (product, sales, engineering)
- Self

Compile, discuss, set goals for the next year. This is when promotion decisions happen.

---

## When the rhythm slips

It will. Customers will demand emergency attention. Trainers will travel. Operators will get sick.

**The rule:** miss one of the three weekly meetings, no problem. Miss two in a row — that's an early signal that something's wrong. Miss all three — the trainer is failing the operator. Reset.

The Academy works because the rhythm is steady. Steady beats heroic every time.
