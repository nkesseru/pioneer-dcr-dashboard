# 02 — Competency Model

Seven pillars. Each pillar has 3–5 competencies. Each competency has four progression levels. This is the map.

## How to read this

Every competency is rated **0 / 1 / 2 / 3**:

- **0 — Unaware.** Doesn't know the concept exists, or has wrong mental model.
- **1 — Aware.** Can describe it; cannot do it.
- **2 — Competent.** Can do it solo on a typical customer with supervision available.
- **3 — Expert.** Can do it solo on a strategic customer, AND can teach it to someone at level 1.

We aim for **all competencies at level 2 by graduation**, with at least 5 competencies at level 3. We do NOT aim for level 3 across the board — that's senior operator work, 12+ months in.

## The Seven Pillars

### Pillar 01 — SaaS Foundations

The customer success craft itself. How modern B2B SaaS companies think about retention, expansion, and the customer lifecycle.

| ID | Competency | Description |
|---|---|---|
| 01.1 | Customer lifecycle | Acquire → activate → adopt → expand → retain (or churn). Operators can name the stage every account is in and what the next gate is. |
| 01.2 | Activation & time-to-value | Define the activation event for a PioneerOps customer (first complete week of clock-ins + first DCR + first QBR scheduled). Measure TTV and own it. |
| 01.3 | Retention mechanics | NRR vs GRR vs NDR. Why each matters. What drives each. How to read the numbers without lying to yourself. |
| 01.4 | Health scoring | Quantitative signals + qualitative signals. Building a usable health model and calibrating it against actual outcomes. |
| 01.5 | Renewal motion | Surfacing risk early. Pre-renewal sequences. The math of a save vs a discount. |

### Pillar 02 — Cleaning Operations

The customer's business. Operators who skip this become forgettable vendors who happen to talk to cleaning companies.

| ID | Competency | Description |
|---|---|---|
| 02.1 | Service models | Recurring nightly routes vs day porter vs specialty (post-construction, deep clean, supply restock). Pricing patterns for each. |
| 02.2 | Labor economics | Wage structure (hourly vs salary, W-2 vs 1099). Productivity benchmarks. Why Revenue Per Labor Hour is the single most important number. |
| 02.3 | Quality systems | Inspections, DCRs, customer feedback loops. The difference between a company that inspects and one that doesn't. |
| 02.4 | Billing patterns | Monthly recurring vs per-occurrence. AR aging realities (most cleaning customers pay 35–55 days). Recurring revenue base as the company's floor. |
| 02.5 | Operational pain points | Common failure modes: callouts, no-shows, supply runs, customer complaints, key/access problems, rotating route managers. |

### Pillar 03 — PioneerOps Platform Mastery

The product itself. Operators must know every workflow, every screen, every common bug — better than the customer ever will.

| ID | Competency | Description |
|---|---|---|
| 03.1 | Core data model | Techs, customers, service assignments, sessions. The relationships between them. What "active" means in each context. |
| 03.2 | Time tracking + payroll | Clock in/out flows. Service sessions. Payroll periods. Time adjustment requests. The Verification Layer. |
| 03.3 | Inspections + DCR | Inspection v2.1 scoring. DCR submission. Customer Quality Score. Cadence + due dates. |
| 03.4 | Communication threads | Categories, priorities, message types, the five-state machine. SMS transport. When to use in-app vs SMS. |
| 03.5 | Mission Control | CEO Actions, Leadership Pulse, Customer Economics (RPLH), Financial Pulse, Hiring Funnel. Reading the dashboards without confusion. |
| 03.6 | Admin tools | Roles, customer aliases, payroll exports, deputy bridge, hiring sync, QBO connection. The plumbing. |

### Pillar 04 — AI Operator

Using modern AI tools to do the operator's work — analysis, drafting, building, summarizing — faster than any unassisted operator could.

| ID | Competency | Description |
|---|---|---|
| 04.1 | Prompt patterns | Writing prompts that produce useful first drafts. Iterating prompts. Knowing when AI will help vs when it'll mislead. |
| 04.2 | Analysis with AI | Feeding spreadsheets, exports, or screenshots to Claude/equivalent and getting back honest analysis with caveats. |
| 04.3 | Drafting with AI | QBR decks, customer emails, internal briefs, expansion proposals. Always edited before sending. |
| 04.4 | Building small tools | Quick scripts, formulas, dashboards. A 30-minute Cursor session can replace a week of manual work. |
| 04.5 | AI judgment | Knowing what AI does poorly. Never asking AI to "decide." Always editing factual claims. Never sending unedited AI output to a customer. |

### Pillar 05 — Customer Success

The actual job. Discovery, onboarding, QBRs, expansion, churn-risk conversations.

| ID | Competency | Description |
|---|---|---|
| 05.1 | Discovery | First call. Reading the customer's actual pain (not just stated pain). Setting up the relationship for the next 24 months. |
| 05.2 | Onboarding & implementation | The first 30 days. Data import. Customer roster setup. First-week training. Hand-off from sales clean and complete. |
| 05.3 | QBR delivery | Quarterly business reviews using PioneerOps data. Three recommendations every time. CEO-grade narrative. |
| 05.4 | Renewals & expansion | When to bring up expansion (not at QBR if you're behind on basics). How to position pricing changes. Multi-year contracts. |
| 05.5 | Difficult conversations | Churn-risk save calls. Price-increase conversations. Service-issue accountability without throwing the field team under the bus. |

### Pillar 06 — Revenue Operations

The operator's responsibility for pipeline, pricing, expansion, and forecasting.

| ID | Competency | Description |
|---|---|---|
| 06.1 | Pipeline hygiene | Health-scored accounts, expansion candidates, churn risks. Weekly review. |
| 06.2 | Pricing & packaging | Why our pricing is what it is. Negotiating discounts. Knowing when to walk vs concede. |
| 06.3 | Expansion plays | Multi-location, premium tier, add-on modules. The math behind each. Right time to pitch. |
| 06.4 | Forecasting | NRR forecast, churn forecast, expansion forecast. Honest numbers. No sandbagging, no happy-ear inflation. |

### Pillar 07 — Executive Advisor

Talking to cleaning company owners and CEOs as a peer, not a vendor.

| ID | Competency | Description |
|---|---|---|
| 07.1 | CEO-level conversation | Translating PioneerOps data into language a non-technical owner cares about. Outcome talk, not feature talk. |
| 07.2 | Industry context | Where commercial cleaning is going. Wage pressure, M&A consolidation, customer demand shifts. Operators should read industry publications weekly. |
| 07.3 | Strategic recommendations | Standing across from a CEO and saying "raise rates 8% on these 12 customers" or "drop these 3 customers, you're losing money on them." Backed by data. |
| 07.4 | Pushback & disagreement | When the customer is wrong, saying so respectfully. Operators who only validate are not advisors. |

## The bars

| Certification | Bar |
|---|---|
| Apprentice Operator | All competencies at level 1+ |
| Operator I | 20 competencies at level 2+, no zeros |
| Operator II | All 34 at level 2+, at least 5 at level 3 |
| Senior Operator | All 34 at level 2+, at least 12 at level 3, plus mentoring an Apprentice |
| Operator Lead | All 34 at level 3, plus contributing to product roadmap |
