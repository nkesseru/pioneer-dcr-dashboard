# 06 — Scorecard

What we measure. How we measure it. The bars per tier.

The scorecard tells the operator what "good" looks like in numbers, not opinions.

---

## Two halves

The operator's evaluation has two halves:

1. **What they delivered** — the numbers
2. **How they delivered it** — the judgment, the presence, the way they show up

Both matter.

- Great numbers + bad behavior → cannot promote past Operator II
- Great behavior + bad numbers → gets coaching on the numbers, but probably will not make it

---

## Half 1 — The Numbers

### 1. Net Revenue Retention (NRR) of owned accounts

What it is: renewal revenue + expansion revenue ÷ starting revenue, last 12 months.

| Tier | Bar |
|---|---|
| Apprentice | not applicable |
| Operator I | ≥ 95% |
| Operator II | ≥ 105% |
| Senior | ≥ 115% |
| Lead | ≥ 125% |

### 2. Gross Revenue Retention (GRR)

What it is: renewal revenue ÷ starting revenue. Catches churn even when expansion would hide it.

| Tier | Bar |
|---|---|
| Operator I | ≥ 85% |
| Operator II | ≥ 90% |
| Senior | ≥ 92% |
| Lead | ≥ 95% |

### 3. Customer Health Score average

What it is: the average health score across owned accounts. Scale 0–100.

| Tier | Bar |
|---|---|
| Operator I | ≥ 65 |
| Operator II | ≥ 75 |
| Senior | ≥ 80 |
| Lead | ≥ 85 |

### 4. Time to Value (TTV) on new customer onboardings

What it is: days from contract sign to the activation event.

| Tier | Bar |
|---|---|
| Operator I | ≤ 30 days |
| Operator II | ≤ 21 days |
| Senior | ≤ 14 days |
| Lead | ≤ 10 days |

### 5. QBRs delivered per quarter

| Tier | Bar |
|---|---|
| Operator I | ≥ 3 (small accounts owned) |
| Operator II | ≥ 8 |
| Senior | ≥ 12 (including ≥ 3 strategic-account CEOs) |
| Lead | owns segment QBR cadence |

### 6. Customer NPS (from owned accounts)

What it is: a survey score from customers. Scale -100 to +100. Run quarterly.

| Tier | Bar |
|---|---|
| Operator I | ≥ 30 |
| Operator II | ≥ 50 |
| Senior | ≥ 60 |
| Lead | ≥ 70 |

### 7. Expansion deals closed

What it is: signed expansion contracts per quarter (multi-location, premium tier, add-on modules).

| Tier | Bar |
|---|---|
| Operator I | ≥ 1 per half |
| Operator II | ≥ 1 per quarter |
| Senior | ≥ 2 per quarter |
| Lead | owns segment expansion |

### 8. Churn-risk saves

What it is: an at-risk account that ends up renewing at full or expanded value.

| Tier | Bar |
|---|---|
| Operator I | observe and escalate; no bar |
| Operator II | ≥ 1 per half |
| Senior | ≥ 2 per half (including ≥ 1 strategic) |
| Lead | owns the process |

## Half 2 — The Manager Rating

Rated monthly by the trainer / manager. Scale 1–5.

### 1. Coachability

Takes feedback well. Implements changes. Does not get defensive.

| Score | What it looks like |
|---|---|
| 1 | Defensive. Argues with feedback. Behavior does not change. |
| 2 | Listens. Sometimes acts on feedback. |
| 3 | Implements feedback consistently. |
| 4 | Asks for feedback proactively. Iterates fast. |
| 5 | Other operators learn from how this person takes feedback. |

### 2. Customer empathy

Sees the world from the customer's seat.

| Score | What it looks like |
|---|---|
| 1 | Treats customers as problems to manage. |
| 2 | Knows the playbook. Misses the human. |
| 3 | Reads the customer's mood. Adjusts approach. |
| 4 | Customers say "this person really gets us." |
| 5 | Customer CEOs ask for this operator by name. |

### 3. Executive presence

Holds their own with senior people. Not theatrical. Not deferential.

| Score | What it looks like |
|---|---|
| 1 | Nervous. Defers too much. |
| 2 | Composed. Struggles under pushback. |
| 3 | Confident. Handles hard questions. |
| 4 | Reads the room. Drives the conversation. |
| 5 | Customer CEOs treat them as a peer. |

### 4. AI fluency

Uses AI as a tool without becoming dependent.

| Score | What it looks like |
|---|---|
| 1 | Does not use AI for operator work. |
| 2 | Uses AI occasionally. Copy-pastes output. |
| 3 | Edits AI output. Uses AI for drafts and analysis. |
| 4 | Builds reusable prompts and small tools. |
| 5 | Teaches AI workflows to others. |

### 5. Operational judgment

Knows when something matters and when it does not.

| Score | What it looks like |
|---|---|
| 1 | Often misreads situation severity. |
| 2 | Defers to manager judgment. |
| 3 | Calls it right most of the time. |
| 4 | Reliable judgment under pressure. |
| 5 | Other operators check with this person before deciding. |

### 6. Initiative

Owns problems beyond their assigned accounts.

| Score | What it looks like |
|---|---|
| 1 | Waits for tasks. |
| 2 | Completes assigned work. |
| 3 | Identifies and acts on issues unprompted. |
| 4 | Proposes process improvements. Volunteers for team work. |
| 5 | Builds playbooks others use. |

### 7. Written communication

Half the job is in writing.

| Score | What it looks like |
|---|---|
| 1 | Errors common. Unclear thinking. |
| 2 | Functional. Verbose or vague. |
| 3 | Clear. Direct. Well-structured. |
| 4 | Customers reply with appreciation for the clarity. |
| 5 | Other operators borrow this person's templates. |

## What we DO NOT measure

| Metric | Why we skip it |
|---|---|
| Calls placed | Vanity. 50 mediocre calls beats 5 great calls in this stat. Loses to them in NRR. |
| Hours worked | We pay for outcomes. 35-hour weeks that hit NRR are wins. |
| Tickets opened, comments left | Noise. Not performance. |
| Customer reply count | If the customer doesn't need to reply because the operator anticipated everything, that's a feature, not a problem. |

---

## What the scorecard is NOT

- Not a tool for threatening people.
- Not the input to a firing decision (those use the manager-rating half + business context).
- Not a substitute for a real conversation.

It is a **coaching tool.** The manager and operator look at it together and ask: where do we lean in this week?

If it gets used to threaten, something has gone wrong upstream.
