# 03 — Certification Ladder

Five tiers. Each tier is a real promotion (in title, scope, and compensation). Operators climb when they meet the bar — not on a calendar.

## The five tiers

| Tier | Typical timeline | Account scope | Supervision |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Apprentice Operator** | Weeks 1–4 | None — shadowing only | Heavy. Pair with a Senior. |
| **Operator I** | Months 2–3 | 3–5 small accounts (<$X ACV) | Weekly 1:1, ad-hoc support |
| **Operator II** | Months 4–9 | 8–12 mid-tier accounts | Biweekly 1:1, independent |
| **Senior Operator** | Months 10–18+ | 12–18 accounts incl. 2–3 strategic | Monthly 1:1, mentoring expected |
| **Operator Lead** | 18+ months | Segment ownership (e.g., all healthcare customers) | Peer-level, contributes to roadmap |

Replace ACV thresholds with real Pioneer numbers once we have them locked. The point isn't the dollar amount — it's that the scope of accountability grows with each tier.

## Apprentice Operator

The first 30 days. Don't touch a customer alone.

### Bar to start

Hired. That's it. We don't expect anything on day one.

### Bar to clear

- Pillar 01 (SaaS Foundations) — all competencies at level 1+
- Pillar 02 (Cleaning Operations) — all competencies at level 1+ AND completed one full overnight cleaning shift
- Pillar 03 (PioneerOps Platform) — all competencies at level 1+, can navigate every screen
- Pillar 04 (AI Operator) — 04.1 and 04.5 at level 1+
- Shadowed at least 5 customer calls (kickoff, QBR, escalation, renewal, expansion)
- Passed Apprentice quiz (in `templates/quiz.md` format)

### What they can do

- Take notes on customer calls
- Update internal systems
- Draft (not send) customer emails for review
- Build internal reports for the manager
- Ask hard questions in safe environments

### What they cannot do

- Lead a customer call
- Send customer comms without review
- Make commitments on Pioneer's behalf
- Run a configuration change in production

### Compensation signal

Standard starting salary for the role. No variable comp yet.

---

## Operator I

Months 2–3. Owns small accounts, supervised by Senior.

### Bar to clear (from Apprentice)

- 20 of 34 competencies at level 2+
- No competencies at level 0
- Pillar 03 (PioneerOps) — all competencies at level 2+ (must own the product)
- Completed first solo customer kickoff (small account)
- Passed Operator I roleplay scenarios (3 of 4)
- Manager rating ≥ 3/5 on all qualitative metrics

### What they can do

- Own 3–5 small accounts end to end
- Run kickoff for small accounts
- Run monthly check-ins independently
- Deliver QBRs to small-account stakeholders (not CEOs yet)
- Send customer comms after weekly review (not pre-review per email)

### What they cannot do

- Own strategic accounts
- Negotiate contract changes
- Make pricing decisions
- Run escalations alone

### Compensation signal

Variable comp opens — small portion tied to retention of owned accounts.

---

## Operator II

Months 4–9. The standard graduate operator. The Academy's primary target.

### Bar to clear (from Operator I)

- All 34 competencies at level 2+
- At least 5 competencies at level 3
- Pillars 03 (Platform) AND 05 (Customer Success) — every competency at level 2+, at least 2 in each at level 3
- Delivered ≥ 8 QBRs with manager scoring ≥ 4/5
- Owns 8–12 accounts with combined health score ≥ 75 (out of 100)
- Closed at least one expansion deal solo
- Saved at least one churn-risk account
- Passed Operator II certification: end-to-end implementation case study + executive briefing exercise

### What they can do

- Own a portfolio of 8–12 mid-tier accounts
- Deliver QBRs to customer CEOs
- Negotiate routine contract terms (renewal pricing, term length, payment terms)
- Run their own expansion plays
- Lead escalations with engineering or product support
- Run AI-assisted analysis end-to-end

### What they cannot do (yet)

- Strategic-account ownership ($100K+ ACV, multi-location, board-level relationships)
- Mentor an Apprentice formally (informal coaching is fine)
- Make product roadmap commitments

### Compensation signal

Full variable comp structure: retention, expansion, NRR — typical SaaS CSM mix.

---

## Senior Operator

Months 10–18+. Trusted with strategic accounts. Starts mentoring.

### Bar to clear (from Operator II)

- All 34 competencies at level 2+
- At least 12 competencies at level 3
- Pillar 07 (Executive Advisor) — every competency at level 3
- Delivered ≥ 3 strategic-account QBRs to CEO-level audiences with NPS score from customer ≥ 60
- Successfully mentored at least one Apprentice through Operator I
- Owns at least one of: multi-location customer, $100K+ ACV customer, customer with board-level relationship
- Contributed at least one Academy improvement (a documented playbook, a fix to a curriculum gap, a new template)

### What they can do

- Everything Operator II can
- Own strategic accounts solo
- Mentor Apprentices and Operator I officially
- Run pricing exception conversations with leadership backing
- Sit in on prospect calls (sales support role)
- Contribute to product roadmap prioritization

### Compensation signal

Senior CSM band. Mentoring stipend or equivalent.

---

## Operator Lead

18+ months. Owns a segment. Peer-level with Pioneer leadership.

### Bar to clear (from Senior Operator)

- All 34 competencies at level 3
- Successfully graduated at least 2 Apprentices to Operator I
- Owns segment-level retention and expansion outcomes (e.g., all healthcare verticals, all multi-location accounts)
- Demonstrated strategic contribution: identified a market trend, championed a product change, or built a new playbook used by ≥ 3 other operators
- Customer CEOs cite this person by name as a key reason they stay

### What they can do

- Own a segment of the customer base, including segment NRR target
- Hire and shape the next cohort of Apprentices (with leadership oversight)
- Sit on the product roadmap committee
- Represent Pioneer at industry events
- Negotiate strategic-account renewals at $200K+ ACV

### Compensation signal

Lead band. Equity refresh. Path to VP CS as the team scales.

## How promotions actually happen

Three signals must align for a promotion:

1. **The scorecard.** The numbers are in [06 — Scorecard](./06-scorecard.md). They must hit.
2. **The artifacts.** Real recorded QBRs, real implementation case studies. See [07 — Graduation Standards](./07-graduation-standards.md).
3. **The judgment call.** Their manager and one external customer reference both believe they're ready.

Don't promote on time-in-seat. Don't promote because someone is bored. Don't promote to retain — if they need a promotion to stay and the bar isn't met, they don't stay.

## Demotions (yes, they happen)

A Senior Operator who isn't mentoring is an Operator II with extra pay. If the manager sees signal that a promotion was premature, raise it within 60 days and either coach them up or move them back. The Academy must protect the integrity of each tier or none of them mean anything.
