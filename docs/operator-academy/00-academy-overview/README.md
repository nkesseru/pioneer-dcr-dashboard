## 1. Academy Mission

> Train Customer Success Managers who can walk into any commercial cleaning company and help them transform operations — using PioneerOps, modern AI, and the kind of operational judgment most people take a decade to build.

We are training **operators**, not account managers.

### What we mean by "operator"

An operator is someone who:

- **Owns outcomes**, not activities. They don't measure success in calls placed; they measure it in customer health going up, RPLH going up, churn going down.
- **Knows the customer's business** as well as they know our software. A Pioneer operator knows what a 15-stop route costs to run, why a janitorial closet matters, and what a typical commercial cleaning customer's gross margin actually is.
- **Uses AI as a force multiplier**, not a curiosity. They draft QBR decks with Claude, build customer-health spreadsheets with Cursor, and run analytical work in 20 minutes that used to take a day.
- **Talks to CEOs like a peer**, not a vendor. They can sit across from the owner of a 200-employee cleaning company and explain why their Revenue Per Labor Hour is $11 below target — and what to do about it.

### What we are NOT training

- ❌ Generic SaaS CSMs who run quarterly check-ins and escalate everything else
- ❌ Implementation consultants who hand off and disappear
- ❌ Technical trainers who know the software but can't have a strategic conversation
- ❌ Salespeople hiding under a CSM title
- ❌ Order-takers who wait for the customer to ask for something

### What a graduate operator can do (the bar)

After completing the Academy (typically 90 days for the first cohort, 60 days once the curriculum matures), an operator can:

1. Run a full customer kickoff for a new PioneerOps deployment — including data migration, role setup, customer roster import, and first-week training.
2. Deliver a 30-minute Quarterly Business Review to a customer CEO using actual PioneerOps data, with three concrete recommendations.
3. Build a customer health model in a spreadsheet, populate it from PioneerOps + their own observation, and use it to predict churn risk 30+ days ahead.
4. Run a difficult conversation — a price increase, a churn-risk save, a service-level complaint — without escalating.
5. Use Claude or equivalent AI tooling to compress a day of analytical work into 30 minutes.
6. Propose an expansion play (new module, additional location, premium tier) with the ROI math worked out.
7. Be the smartest person in the room when the customer's CEO asks "how should I think about labor cost as a percentage of revenue?"

### Why this matters to Pioneer

Customer Success isn't a department; it's the company's growth engine.

- **Every retained customer** is worth multi-year revenue. One save funds a real part of an operator's salary.
- **Every expanded account** adds up over time. NRR above 120% is the difference between a good SaaS company and a great one.
- **Every advocate** brings referrals — the cheapest way to grow a vertical SaaS company.
- **Every operator insight** sharpens the product. Operators in the field hear what customers need before the customer files a ticket.

If we get this right, the Academy is the highest-leverage hire training program at Pioneer — not because CSMs are the most expensive role, but because they're the most catalytic.

### The mission in one sentence

> Build the operator bench that makes PioneerOps customers more successful than they would be with any other software, any other consultant, or any other vendor.
