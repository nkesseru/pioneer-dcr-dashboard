# 01 — Who This Is For

The Academy works for the right person and is wasted on the wrong one. Be honest about the profile before hiring.

## Four candidate profiles

We've seen four kinds of person succeed in this role. Any of them can graduate the Academy. Look for these patterns when recruiting.

### Profile A — The Operations Veteran

A current or recent operations manager from a commercial cleaning, janitorial, facilities, or similar field services company. 5+ years in the industry. They know what a 4 AM start looks like, they've fired underperforming techs, and they've explained an invoice line item to an angry customer.

- **Strengths:** Credibility with customer CEOs. Operational instinct. No translation layer needed.
- **Where they struggle:** SaaS metrics (NRR, activation, churn modeling). Comfort with AI tools. Quarterly cadence — they're used to daily firefighting.
- **Curriculum emphasis:** Pillars 01 (SaaS) and 04 (AI). Pillar 02 (Cleaning Ops) is a refresher, not a class.

### Profile B — The SaaS CSM

A 2–5 year veteran of a customer success role at another B2B SaaS company. They know health scoring, quarterly business reviews, expansion playbooks. They've never set foot in a commercial cleaning building.

- **Strengths:** Process discipline. Customer comms craft. Activation metrics. Comfort with software.
- **Where they struggle:** Cleaning industry context. Talking to a non-technical owner. Operational empathy — they may underestimate how hard it is to actually do the work the customer does.
- **Curriculum emphasis:** Pillar 02 (Cleaning Ops) goes deep. Mandatory ride-along: one full shift cleaning a building. Pillar 07 (Executive Advisor) needs work.

### Profile C — The High-Coachability Generalist

Junior. 1–3 years total experience. Could be from any background — startup ops, consulting, customer support, even a non-traditional path. What makes them work: they learn fast, they take feedback well, they're genuinely curious about both businesses (commercial cleaning AND SaaS).

- **Strengths:** Plasticity. No bad habits. Hungry. Often more comfortable with AI tools than the seniors.
- **Where they struggle:** Credibility with customer CEOs at first. Needs strong shadowing to develop pattern recognition. Cannot be hired solo until pipeline is robust.
- **Curriculum emphasis:** All pillars equally. Heavy supervised exposure. Pair with a Senior Operator for the first 90 days.

### Profile D — The Internal Rotation

An existing Pioneer team member rotating through the CSM role for 6–12 months. Could be a tech who wants to grow into a desk role, an admin who wants more customer exposure, or a senior staffer who'll eventually own a product area but needs customer empathy.

- **Strengths:** Knows the product cold. Knows Pioneer culture. No onboarding overhead.
- **Where they struggle:** May lack outside-customer empathy. Has internal politics baggage. Sometimes too close to product to hear customer feedback objectively.
- **Curriculum emphasis:** Pillars 01 (SaaS) and 05 (Customer Success). Light treatment of Pillar 03 (Platform Mastery) — they already know it.

## Who this is NOT for

Be ruthless about disqualifying. Hiring the wrong person here costs more than leaving the seat empty.

### ❌ The pure salesperson

If they got into CS to "talk to customers without quota pressure" they will not do this job. Operators have quota — it's just measured in retention and expansion, not new logos.

### ❌ The pure technician

If they want to spend their day in spreadsheets and configs, this is the wrong role. Operators are CEO-facing 30–50% of the week.

### ❌ The career account manager

If their idea of CS is "schedule the quarterly check-in, ask if everything's okay, send the renewal contract" — they will get eaten alive by a commercial cleaning company owner who is paying real money and wants real value.

### ❌ Anyone who needs a fixed job description

This role changes every quarter for the first two years. The platform evolves, the customer base evolves, the playbooks evolve. People who want certainty about exactly what they'll do every day will be miserable.

### ❌ Anyone who can't ride along

Mandatory: spend one full overnight shift cleaning a commercial building before the end of week two. If a candidate refuses this on principle, they don't get the job. We can't have operators who haven't done the work.

## Prerequisites (what we expect on day one)

- Comfortable in spreadsheets — pivot tables, basic formulas, lookups. We'll teach modeling; we won't teach Excel basics.
- Comfortable in a video call as a leader, not a participant.
- Comfortable writing — half the job is written customer comms.
- Curious about commercial cleaning specifically. If they ask "wait, this is for cleaning companies?" with a disappointed tone, end the interview.
- Coachable. Test this in the interview by giving them a piece of feedback in the conversation and seeing how they respond.

## Prerequisites (what we'll teach)

- All SaaS terminology and frameworks
- PioneerOps from zero
- AI tools (Claude, Cursor, etc.) — we'll start with prompt patterns and build from there
- Commercial cleaning operational depth
- Executive presence in customer conversations

## A blunt note on hiring pace

Hire one. Train them through full graduation before hiring the second. The Academy gets sharper with every cohort, but the first graduate IS the trainer for the second. If you hire two simultaneously without anyone to coach them, you'll have two confused operators and two damaged customer relationships.

Wait until graduate #1 is delivering QBRs solo before you post the next req.
