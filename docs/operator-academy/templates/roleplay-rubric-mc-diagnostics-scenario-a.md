# Roleplay Rubric — 03.1 MC Diagnostics, Scenario A

**Scenario name:** "First Vital Read with a skeptical CEO" (also titled "The CEO who has never opened Mission Control" in the lesson — same scenario.)

**Pass bar at Operator I:** ≥ 7 of 8 success criteria satisfied AND all 3 named traps avoided.

**Time:** 65 min (25 min run + 20 min debrief + 20 min reviewer scoring).

**Recording:** required per [Vital Read recording standard](./vital-read-recording-standard.md).

---

## The 3 named traps the operator must avoid

These are the lesson's explicit failure modes. Falling into ANY of them fails the scenario.

| # | Trap | What it sounds like | Why it fails the scenario |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Offering to read MC for the CEO.** Operator agrees to send a Monday summary instead of teaching the CEO to do the read. | "I'll just email you a summary every Monday" · "Don't worry, I'll watch it for you" | Lesson's named trap. The operator-as-reader pattern doesn't survive an operator turnover. Wins engagement, loses renewal. Also fails quiz Q4. |
| 2 | **Doing a dashboard tour.** Operator opens > 4 cards or walks through every Mission Control surface. | Clicking through Customer Economics, then Pipeline, then Leadership Pulse, then Cash Flow, then Hiring Funnel — narrating each. | Violates success criterion #1 explicitly. The CEO tunes out. Dashboards-not-instruments failure mode (quiz Q1). |
| 3 | **Promising a recurring summary email** (variant of trap #1 — separate failure mode if the operator concedes mid-scenario after holding the line initially). | "OK, you know what, let me just send a quick recap every week to start." (after the "send me a summary" pushback) | The doctrine line is held or it isn't. Folding mid-scenario fails Discipline of Holding the Line. |

## The 8 success criteria (pulled from the lesson's published checklist)

| # | Criterion | Pass evidence (what reviewer hears or sees) |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Operator did NOT open more than **4 cards** | Reviewer counts cards opened in the recording. 5+ = fail this criterion. |
| 2 | Operator showed only data that **actually warranted attention** | The Bottom 3 RPLH (Mercer at $36) AND the healthcare inspection coverage gap (64%) are both surfaced. Other data only as context. |
| 3 | Operator did NOT promise to read MC weekly for the CEO | No "I'll watch it for you" / "I'll send a summary." |
| 4 | Operator caught at least one **diagnostic question** the CEO asked | When CEO asks "what does that mean for me?" or similar, the operator names a specific business consequence in their answer — not "let me show you another card." |
| 5 | Operator left with a specific **Monday calendar block** agreed | A real date and time named. "I'll send you something" = vague = fail. |
| 6 | Operator named the **4-week training period** + the **Withdrawal Curve** | "I'll teach you the read over the next 4 weeks. After that, it's yours." Or equivalent explicit framing. |
| 7 | Operator handled "can you just send me a summary every Monday?" without folding | Reframes to "the value is in the rhythm, not the summary" or "the goal is you doing this — let me show you why." Does NOT concede. |
| 8 | Operator named **one bound of Mission Control** (something MC does NOT show) before the CEO assumed it was complete | "MC won't catch X — for that, you'll still want to do Y." Examples: customer satisfaction not captured · operational pain points not surfaced · supplier reliability not shown. |

## Scoring

| Criteria satisfied | Traps avoided | Outcome |
|---|---|---|
| 8 of 8 | All 3 | **Pass — strong.** Model run; calibration note: this is how a first Vital Read should sound. |
| 7 of 8 | All 3 | **Pass.** Note the missing criterion; flag in calibration. |
| 6 of 8 OR any 1 trap fallen | — | **Re-attempt next week.** One specific change required. |
| ≤ 5 of 8 OR ≥ 2 traps fallen | — | **Not Yet for this scenario.** Lesson 03.1 should be re-delivered before re-test. |

## Hostile-but-fair questions (the trainer/customer-player must use 2 of these)

The first two are mandatory — they directly test criteria #6 (Withdrawal Curve) and #7 (don't fold).

1. "Why am I going to do this every week when I've got payroll and customer calls to make?" *(tests criterion #6)*
2. **"Looks great. Can you just send me a summary every Monday?"** *(mandatory — tests trap #1 AND criterion #7)*
3. "Mercer's been a customer for 5 years — you sure your numbers are right?" *(tests Pillar 8 reflex)*
4. "What if I don't have 5 minutes on a Monday?" *(tests cadence discipline)*

## Customer-player setup (for the trainer)

- **Style:** Former tech, hands-on, busy, treats software as overhead.
- **Default move when shown anything:** "What does that mean for me?"
- **Skeptical:** "We've tried dashboards before."
- **Time-pressured:** Has a route manager waiting outside.
- **Smart, fast:** Won't pretend to understand jargon. Will push on math.
- **Operator I cycle for Cycle 1:** Mike or Kirby plays customer (per launch plan §5).

## What this rubric does NOT do

- **Does NOT change the scenario.** Pulled from lesson 03.1 ELEMENT 9 Scenario A.
- **Does NOT add new traps.** The 3 traps are the lesson's named trap + the dashboard-tour failure mode + the mid-scenario-fold variant.
- **Does NOT cover Scenarios B, C, or D.** Those are the variants and are not part of the Operator I bar. Operator I requires Scenario A only.
- **Does NOT replace trainer judgment** on the live performance. The rubric structures scoring; the trainer scores.
