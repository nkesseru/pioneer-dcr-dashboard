# Trainer Guide Template

The coach's companion to a lesson. What to watch for, how to score, common pitfalls. One guide per lesson. Lives alongside the lesson plan.

The lesson plan is for the operator. The trainer guide is for you.

---

## Paired with lesson

> Path to the lesson plan, e.g. `01-saas-foundations/02-activation-and-ttv.md`

## Time investment for the trainer

> Prep: 30 min — Lesson: 90 min — Debrief: 30 min — Post-write-up: 15 min — Total: 2h45m

## Before the lesson — what to prepare

- [ ] Pre-work sent 24+ hours ago, confirmed received
- [ ] Real customer artifact identified and anonymized
- [ ] Recording setup if relevant
- [ ] Spot-check questions ready (3–5)
- [ ] Common-pitfall list reviewed (see below)
- [ ] You can answer the operator's pre-work question

If you can't answer the operator's pre-work question, push the lesson by a day and figure it out. Don't fake.

---

## Coaching cues during the lesson

| Signal | What it might mean | What to do |
|---|---|---|
| Operator stops asking questions | Lost, defensive, or zoned out | Pause. Ask "what's the most confusing thing so far?" |
| Operator asks edge-case question early | Avoiding the harder fundamental | Acknowledge, defer, return to the core |
| Operator nods a lot without writing | Pretending to follow | Stop. Ask them to summarize in their own words |
| Operator pushes back on a concept | Either engaged or threatened | Engage the pushback. Ask "what makes you skeptical?" |
| Operator finishes the exercise too fast | They skipped a step or didn't go deep | Review their output before celebrating |
| Operator can't get started on the exercise | Confidence issue or genuine knowledge gap | Sit beside them; do the first step together |

## Spot-check questions

The operator should be able to answer at least 3 of these by end of lesson. Pick three for the actual session.

1. [Concept Q]
2. [Concept Q]
3. [Worked-example Q]
4. [Scenario Q]
5. [Edge-case Q]

> Example for activation lesson:
> 1. What's the activation event for a PioneerOps customer?
> 2. Why does activation drive 12-month retention more than any other early signal?
> 3. Show me the dashboard you'd use Monday morning to check who's at risk of stalling.

## How to grade the exercise

| Criterion | Bar to pass |
|---|---|
| Output completeness | All required sections present |
| Output accuracy | Numbers correct; concepts applied correctly |
| Reasoning visible | Operator can defend each choice |
| Time on task | Not too fast (skipped) or too slow (lost) |
| One concrete next step | Operator identifies how they'll apply this |

Operator passes the lesson if 4 of 5 are met. If 3 of 5 — repeat the exercise next week. If 2 or fewer — repeat the lesson; flag for trainer review.

## Common pitfalls (operator-side)

Updated every cohort. After teaching this lesson three times, you'll know the patterns.

- **Pitfall A:** Operator confuses [X] with [Y]
  - **How to spot:** They use the terms interchangeably
  - **Fix:** Worked example that forces the distinction
- **Pitfall B:** Operator over-indexes on [tool/feature]
  - **How to spot:** They want to talk about [thing] when [other thing] is the real lever
  - **Fix:** Redirect with the question "what would the customer care about?"
- **Pitfall C:** Operator skips the math
  - **How to spot:** They give qualitative recommendations without ROI math
  - **Fix:** Don't accept a recommendation without numbers

## Common pitfalls (trainer-side)

You'll make these too. Watch for them.

- **Teaching too long.** Operator checks out after 30 minutes of straight content. Break it up.
- **Skipping the worked example.** It's tempting to "just explain it." The worked example is where the learning happens.
- **Grading the exercise too generously.** If you don't push back, the operator doesn't improve.
- **Not connecting the lesson to the operator's accounts.** Always tie the lesson to a real account they own.
- **Letting recordings die in a folder.** Watch them. Review with the operator. Pick one improvement per recording.

---

## Calibration with other trainers

If multiple people teach this lesson:

- Score 3 operator exercises and compare ratings. Adjust the rubric if you're 1+ point apart.
- Watch each other's recorded lessons quarterly.
- Maintain a shared "common pitfalls" doc that gets updated as patterns emerge.
