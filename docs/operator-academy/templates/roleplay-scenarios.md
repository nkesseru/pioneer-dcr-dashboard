# Roleplay Scenarios Template

The catalog of scenarios used in Wednesday skill drills + Operator I / II certifications.

Each scenario is a self-contained brief: customer setup, the operator's task, the trainer's role, the debrief structure. Copy this file, rename per scenario, fill in.

A great scenario library is one of Pioneer's most valuable internal assets. Add to it every time a real customer situation teaches you something worth practicing.

## The trigger

What just happened. The reason this conversation is happening now.

> Example: *The customer's CEO emailed at 11pm last night: "Can we talk this week? Margins are tight and I need to think about our PioneerOps spend."*

---

## The operator's task

What we're asking the operator to do, in one sentence.

> Example: *Run a 20-minute call with the CEO. Save the renewal. Set up a 30-day plan.*

---

## The trainer's role

How the trainer plays this scenario.

| Trainer plays | Style | Pushback points |
|---|---|---|
| The CEO | Direct, owner-operator, no patience for fluff | Will challenge the operator's data, will probe the value of PioneerOps, will threaten to leave |

Trainer notes for staying in character:

- Don't fold. The operator's job is to navigate pushback, not to face a friendly audience.
- Don't be unrealistically hostile either. CEOs are reasonable; they want real value.
- Use real cleaning-industry phrases. ("My route managers can't onboard another system." "I'm watching my labor cost like a hawk.")
- If the operator says something that would ring false to a real CEO, react authentically: "I don't follow you. What does that mean for me?"

---

## The trap

What the scenario is designed to test. Operators must demonstrate they don't fall in.

> Example: *The trap is to defend PioneerOps' value with feature talk. The operator who lists features loses. The operator who reframes the conversation to the CEO's actual outcome — RPLH gap, margin compression — wins.*

---

## Success criteria

What "passing" this scenario looks like.

- [ ] Operator did NOT [trap action]
- [ ] Operator translated PioneerOps value into the CEO's language
- [ ] Operator held a position when challenged (didn't fold)
- [ ] Operator left the call with a concrete next step
- [ ] Operator finished within the time budget
- [ ] Operator did NOT make commitments outside their authority

---

## Debrief structure (15 min)

| Time | Activity |
|---|---|
| 0:00–0:03 | Operator self-rates: what worked, what didn't |
| 0:03–0:08 | Trainer feedback (one keep, one change) |
| 0:08–0:13 | Replay the inflection point — the moment the conversation could have gone either way |
| 0:13–0:15 | Commit: one thing the operator will apply next week |

The most important moment of any roleplay is the replay. Find the inflection point and re-run it with a better approach.

---

## Variations (for re-runs)

After the operator passes the base scenario, run variations:

### Variation A — friendlier CEO

Same setup, but the CEO is open and curious. Operator must now identify expansion, not just save.

### Variation B — hostile CEO

Same setup, but the CEO has already decided to leave and is just informing you. Can you reset the conversation?

### Variation C — confused CEO

Same setup, but the CEO doesn't really understand the data. Operator must teach without condescending.

Run the same scenario 3–4 times across a cohort. The operator who can handle all variations is at level 3.
