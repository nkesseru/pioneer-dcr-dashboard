# Foundation Quiz Answer Keys — Operator I Grader's Sheet

This is the single grader's instrument for the 4 foundation lesson quizzes. Each quiz is **10 questions, 1 point each, pass = 7/10**.

**Source of truth:** the answers below are pulled verbatim from each lesson's published quiz section. If the grader and the lesson disagree, the **lesson** wins — flag the discrepancy and update this sheet, not the lesson.

**Operator I bar:** ≥ 7/10 on at least 3 of the 4 quizzes (per framework §2).

---

## Scoring rules (apply to all 4 quizzes)

| Question type | Scoring rule |
|---|---|
| **Multiple choice (MCQ)** | 1 point for the correct letter. 0 for anything else. No partial credit. |
| **Math / calculation** | 1 point for correct number AND correct sign / unit. 0.5 for arithmetic error but right approach. 0 if approach is wrong. |
| **Short answer (1–2 sentences)** | 1 point if the named criteria below are present. 0.5 for partial. 0 if the doctrine-level point is missed. |
| **Judgment / scenario** | Score against the criteria listed below. If the criteria include a rubric (Q10 of lessons 03.1 and 05.3), use the rubric verbatim. |

**Half points round down at the end.** A candidate with 6.5/10 scores 6, not 7. The Operator I bar is strict.

**Retake policy:** if a quiz scores < 7, the candidate may retake it once. Second attempt < 7 = a category gap for the reviewer scorecard. No third attempts in Cycle 1.

## Lesson 02.3 — Customer Economics ([source](../02-cleaning-operations/03-customer-economics.md))

| Q | Type | Correct answer | Pass criteria / what to look for | Common wrong answer |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Q1 | MCQ | **b** (gross profit per customer — revenue minus direct labor, burden, supplies) | Just the letter. Customer Economics stops at gross profit; excludes overhead. | a (net profit), c (revenue minus all overhead) — both include overhead |
| Q2 | Math | Direct labor $1,760 · Burden $528 · Supplies $200 · **Gross Profit $3,512** · **Gross Margin 58.5%** | Right total AND right margin. Common error: forgetting burden. | Skipping burden → GP $4,040 (wrong) |
| Q3 | MCQ | **c** (Customer B requires more labor and/or supplies for same revenue) | Just the letter. Same revenue, different costs = different margins. | a, b, d miss the cost-side framing |
| Q4 | Short answer | 5.5 hrs/month × $21.76 loaded wage = **$119.68/month** (~$1,440/year annualized) | Operator includes burden. Math is right. Annualizing is a bonus. | Forgetting burden = $93.50/month (wrong magnitude) |
| Q5 | MCQ (pick all) | **a, b, d, f** (payroll taxes, workers' comp, PTO accrual, sick leave accrual) | Must select all 4 correct AND no wrong. Burden = scales with labor hours. | Including c (office rent) or e (owner salary) = both are overhead, not burden |
| Q6 | Short answer | Names: low-margin new customers OR scope creep + rising wages/supplies. Revenue up + margin down = total gross profit can fall. | Understanding that revenue growth and profit growth are different directions. | "They added more customers" (incomplete — misses the margin-mix story) |
| Q7 | MCQ | **b** (push owners to add more customers, deepening the problem) | Just the letter. Natural response (find more revenue) makes scope-creep companies worse. | a (self-corrects) — directly contradicts the lesson |
| Q8 | Short answer | "After you pay your team and buy supplies, how much of each dollar from a customer is left? That number is your Customer Economics." | Plain English. No "EBITDA," no "burden-loaded," no "margin compression." | Uses any jargon term = fail |
| Q9 | MCQ | **b** (Customer Economics stops at gross margin; customers unprofitable in CE may still be net profitable at company level) | Just the letter. Precise language earns trust. | Any other letter shows misunderstanding |
| Q10 | Judgment | Gross Margin = **61%**. Operator's move: do NOT push rate increase. Customer is fine. Conversation moves elsewhere. | Operator does the math FIRST. Doesn't assume "old customer" = "bad customer." CE tells the truth either way. | "Push for a rate increase anyway" (ignores the math) |

## Lesson 03.1 — Mission Control Diagnostics ([source](../03-pioneerops-platform-mastery/01-mission-control-diagnostics.md))

| Q | Type | Correct answer | Pass criteria / what to look for | Common wrong answer |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Q1 | Short answer | Dashboard = looked at. Diagnostic instrument = read by someone trained to interpret it. Discipline of reading produces action; act of looking produces glancing. | Operator names the verb difference (look vs read) AND the discipline difference (trained interpretation vs casual observation). | "A dashboard shows data, an instrument is more important" (misses the verb/discipline distinction) |
| Q2 | MCQ | **c** (Action Completion Loop + Leadership Streak — serves Verification) | Just the letter. Operator holds the Loop Map cold. | Mixing up Action and Verification surfaces = doctrinal failure |
| Q3 | Short answer | **5 minutes.** | Operator knows the time bound. | "As long as it takes" = has not internalized the read's discipline |
| Q4 | MCQ | **c** (decline — explain that operator-as-reader is fragile; goal is CEO becoming self-sustaining) | Just the letter. Operator-as-reader produces customers who lose diagnostic capability the moment operator changes. | a or b — both leave the operator-as-reader pattern in place |
| Q5 | Short answer | Pause. Verify the underlying records before acting. Confirm no time-card sync gap / no recent assignment change / no record incompleteness. If records check out, proceed. If not, fix the record first. | Operator pauses on principle. Does NOT act on an MC number without verifying records beneath it. | "Send the rate increase letter now" (Pillar 8 violation) |
| Q6 | Short answer | Time should **decrease**. Value should **increase**. Reads become CEO's job. Operator's attention reallocates to harder strategic calls the reads surface. | Operator understands time + value move in opposite directions on a healthy account, not the same direction. | "Both go up" or "both go down" (misses the Withdrawal Curve) |
| Q7 | MCQ | **b** (field behavior writing the problem is 30+ days old by time it's caught) | Just the letter. The cadence is the entire economic argument for MC. | a, c, d miss the cadence-as-value point |
| Q8 | Short answer | **Cash Conversion** AND **Revenue Quality & Leakage**. Cash Conversion = time from work-done to cash-received. Revenue Quality catches scope creep, unbilled work, other leaks. | Operator names the two correct vitals AND explains why each one specifically addresses cash worry. | Only naming one vital, or naming wrong vitals (Labor Efficiency etc.) |
| Q9 | MCQ | **c** (customer catches a deviation and runs diagnosis without operator, BEFORE operator's next visit) | Just the letter. The Self-Sustaining test is the customer demonstrably doing diagnostic work without the operator. | a, b, d are all engagement signals, not self-sustaining signals |
| Q10 | Judgment (90-sec compression) | **Use the lesson's inline 5-criterion rubric verbatim.** See [lesson source line ~894](../03-pioneerops-platform-mastery/01-mission-control-diagnostics.md). All 5 criteria required for pass: time (80–95s) · cadence as the value · cost of inaction translated · Withdrawal Curve named · ends with specific calendar ask. | 5 of 5 = pass · 4 of 5 = re-attempt next week · ≤ 3 = lesson re-delivery before re-test. | Going over 95 sec · cadence framed as data instead of rhythm · no specific calendar ask |
