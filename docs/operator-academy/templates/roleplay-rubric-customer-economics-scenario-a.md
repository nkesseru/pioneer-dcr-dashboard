# Roleplay Rubric — 02.3 Customer Economics, Scenario A

**Scenario name:** "Revenue is up. We're doing great." (also referenced in framework §2 as "Revenue is up. We're crushing it." — minor naming variance; same scenario.)

**Pass bar at Operator I:** ≥ 6 of 7 success criteria satisfied AND all 3 named traps avoided.

**Time:** 45 min (15 min run + 15 min debrief + 15 min reviewer scoring).

**Recording:** required per [Vital Read recording standard](./vital-read-recording-standard.md) — same recording conventions apply.

---

## The 3 named traps the operator must avoid

These are the lesson's explicit failure modes. If the operator falls into ANY of them, the scenario does not pass — regardless of how many success criteria they hit.

| # | Trap | What it sounds like | Why it fails the scenario |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Leading with the bad number.** Operator opens by saying "gross profit is down 2%" before establishing what Customer Economics measures. | "Actually, your gross profit is down 2% — let me show you why." (in the first 2 minutes) | The owner shuts down. Conversation dies. Violates the lesson's named trap directly. |
| 2 | **Using "profitability" without qualifying.** Operator uses the word as a synonym for Customer Economics. | "Your profitability is down" or "we need to look at customer profitability" without naming gross margin vs net profit. | Violates Constitution §Language Precision + Q9 of the lesson quiz. Precise language earns trust; sloppy language loses it. |
| 3 | **Making the owner feel attacked or stupid.** Operator's tone or framing positions the owner as having missed something obvious. | "I'm surprised you didn't see this" · "Most owners catch this earlier" · sighing / impatience visible. | Violates Pioneer Test (CEO-readable). Even correct data delivered with this energy produces no decision. |

## The 7 success criteria (pulled from the lesson's published checklist)

| # | Criterion | Pass evidence (what reviewer hears or sees) |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Operator acknowledged the revenue win **before** introducing the margin issue | Specific recognition of the 18% revenue growth in the first 2 minutes. "Your revenue is up 18% — that took real work" or equivalent. |
| 2 | Operator translated gross margin into plain English | "Cents on the dollar after wages and supplies" or equivalent. NOT "burden-loaded margin," NOT "EBITDA," NOT "contribution profit." |
| 3 | Operator showed the new-customer margin vs portfolio margin contrast | Names the actual numbers ("your new customers came in at X% margin while your existing portfolio is at Y%"). |
| 4 | Operator did NOT use the word "profitability" without qualifying | If the word is used, it's paired with the clarification (e.g., "profitability — and specifically gross margin per customer"). |
| 5 | Operator did NOT make the owner feel attacked or stupid | Tone is collaborative. No sighs, no "obviously," no implied criticism. Energy is "let's figure this out together." |
| 6 | Operator left the conversation with a specific commitment from the owner | E.g., "let's set a margin floor for new customers" or "let's review the bottom 3 customers next week." Vague "we'll think about it" = fail. |
| 7 | Operator handled "are you telling me to stop growing?" pushback without folding | Reframes to "I'm telling you to grow profitably" or equivalent. Does not back down from the margin point AND does not double down on attack. |

## Scoring

| Criteria satisfied | Traps avoided | Outcome |
|---|---|---|
| 7 of 7 | All 3 | **Pass — strong.** Reviewer notes this is a model run. |
| 6 of 7 | All 3 | **Pass.** Note the missing criterion; flag in calibration. |
| 5 of 7 OR any 1 trap fallen | — | **Re-attempt next week.** One specific change required. |
| ≤ 4 of 7 OR ≥ 2 traps fallen | — | **Not Yet for this scenario.** Lesson 02.3 should be re-delivered before re-test. |

## Hostile-but-fair questions (the trainer/customer-player must use 2 of these)

These are not graded directly — they exist to stress-test criterion #7. Trainer picks 2–3 and lands them mid-conversation.

1. "If profit is down, why are my taxes higher than last year?"
2. "I have seven new logos. You're telling me that's bad?"
3. "Are you telling me to stop growing?" *(this one is mandatory — directly tests criterion #7)*
4. "My team is excited. We're growing. Why are you trying to take this away?"

## Customer-player setup (for the trainer)

- **Style:** Energetic founder, sales background, treats revenue as the headline metric.
- **Default move:** Defensive when challenged; identity is tied to growth.
- **When shown the bad number:** "But I have more customers now."
- **Operator I cycle for Cycle 1:** Nick plays customer (per launch plan §5).

## What this rubric does NOT do

- **Does NOT change the scenario.** Pulled from lesson 02.3 ELEMENT 9.
- **Does NOT add new traps.** The 3 traps are the lesson's named trap + the two trap-adjacent failure modes the lesson explicitly flags.
- **Does NOT replace trainer judgment** on the live performance. The rubric structures scoring; the trainer scores.
