# Pioneer Test Grading Rubric

The Pioneer Test reflection is a single paragraph written by the candidate, answering: *"Why I think this work passes the Pioneer Test."* This rubric tells the reviewer how to grade that paragraph.

**This is not a rewrite of the Pioneer Test.** The test itself is the Constitution's question: *"Would Nick actually teach it this way?"* This rubric only operationalizes how to grade a candidate's reflection on their own work.

## The 5 criteria

The paragraph passes if **≥ 4 of 5** criteria are explicitly satisfied in the text. A reviewer must be able to point to a specific sentence for each criterion they mark Yes.

| # | Criterion | What it looks like (Yes) | What it looks like (No) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Specificity** — names a real piece of the candidate's own work (named customer or account shorthand, named metric, named action, named date) | "On Acme's Building A, the inspection coverage went from 70% to 91% over the three weeks of June." | "I worked on improving inspection coverage for one of my customers." |
| 2 | **Business-outcome thinking** — names what changed in the customer's business, not what was clicked in PioneerOps | "The customer's RPLH moved from $42 to $47, which added roughly $3,200 to their monthly contribution margin on that route." | "The customer used Mission Control three more times than before." |
| 3 | **Economic Reality reflex** (Pillar 7) — includes a number, math, or measurable element with the arithmetic visible enough to be checked | "Their margin moved from 11% to 14% on a $26K route, which is roughly $780/mo of recovered margin." | "The numbers got better." |
| 4 | **Honesty about failure or uncertainty** — names something that didn't work, didn't move, was uncertain, or that the candidate would do differently | "My second Loop entry — predicting we'd catch 3 more issues with the new inspection cadence — closed as Failure. We caught one. I have a hypothesis about why." | "Everything went well." |
| 5 | **Pioneer voice** — reads like the candidate is talking to a cleaning CEO, not writing a school essay. No generic SaaS jargon (engagement, leverage, synergy). Plain, specific, operational. | "I sat with the GM and we walked the labor stack together. He saw two hours/day he hadn't been tracking." | "We drove cross-functional alignment to leverage operational synergies." |

## Score

| Criteria satisfied (Yes count) | Score |
|---|---|
| 5 of 5 | **Pass — strong.** Reflection is a strength of the evidence pack. |
| 4 of 5 | **Pass.** Meets bar. Note which criterion was missing in the calibration meeting. |
| 3 of 5 | **Conditional.** Candidate must rewrite the paragraph within 7 days, addressing the missing criteria. One rewrite allowed. |
| ≤ 2 of 5 | **Not Yet.** The reflection does not demonstrate Pioneer Test understanding. Cannot certify at this tier. |

## Automatic Not Yet (regardless of criterion count)

The reflection automatically fails if any of these are present:

- **Self-promotional language with no honesty about failure.** ("This work was excellent. Every Loop closed positively.") Violates Pillar 7 honesty standards.
- **Recommends an action without verifying the underlying record.** ("I recommended X based on what the dashboard showed.") Violates Pillar 8.
- **Uses banned vague phrases from Constitution §Language Precision** without rewriting them. ("Drove engagement," "delivered impact," "moved the needle.")
- **Claims a tier the candidate is not certifying at.** ("This is the work of an Operator III.") Confidence-inflation is a structural disqualifier per Pillar 7.

## What this rubric is NOT

- **Not a rewrite of the Pioneer Test.** The test itself remains the Constitution's question.
- **Not subjective.** Reviewers must cite specific text for each Yes/No. Disagreements at calibration are resolved by re-reading the paragraph against the criteria, not by debate.
- **Not a writing-quality rubric.** Grammar and style are not graded. Substance is.

## For the reviewer's scorecard

When filling Category 4 (Portfolio Evidence) row "Pioneer Test reflection" on the [Operator I reviewer scorecard](./operator-i-reviewer-scorecard.md):

- **Pass** → reflection scored Pass or Pass — strong on this rubric
- **N** (does not meet bar) → reflection scored Conditional or Not Yet
- **Missing** → no reflection submitted (auto-disqualifying for intake per the evidence pack index)
