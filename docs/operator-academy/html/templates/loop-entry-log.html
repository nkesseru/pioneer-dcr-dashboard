Loop Entry Log

Every Loop entry is one row. Every row eventually closes — honestly. A row that never gets a verification date filled in is a violation of the Constitution's Loop doctrine and disqualifies the entry as evidence.

Instructions for the candidate

  1. Create a row the moment you make a recommendation to a customer or for your own action. Not later. The row exists before the verification date.
  2. The verification date must be in the future and named. "Next week" is not a date. "2026-07-02" is a date.
  3. On the verification date, you fill in the actual outcome — honestly. Success, partial, or failure. If you don't honor the date, the row counts as an open Loop with no closure (which fails the Operator I bar of "1 fully closed").
  4. You do not delete rows. Failed Loops are evidence of intellectual honesty — the Pioneer Test rewards them.

What "closed" means

A Loop is closed when the verification date has passed AND the actual outcome has been recorded AND the closure status is one of: Success / Partial / Failure. A row with the verification date passed but the actual outcome blank is broken, not closed — and a broken Loop is worse than an open one.

What disqualifies a Loop entry as evidence

  • No baseline measurement (just a hypothesis without a number)
  • Verification date in the past at the moment of creation (retroactive Loops are fabrication)
  • Verification date repeatedly pushed out without explanation
  • "Actual outcome" field never filled even after the date passes
  • Closure note that doesn't acknowledge what was learned — especially in Failure or Partial closures

What reviewers look for

The single closed Loop at Operator I is the doctrine pivot. Reviewers are not impressed by 100 open Loops with no closures. They look for:

  • A real baseline number
  • A real verification date that was honored
  • A closure note that is honest, even when the outcome was bad
  • Evidence the candidate actually went back and checked the record on the verification date

A candidate who shows 3 entries — one Success, one Failure honestly closed, one still Open with a future date — passes this requirement. A candidate who shows 20 entries all Open with vague closure dates fails it.
