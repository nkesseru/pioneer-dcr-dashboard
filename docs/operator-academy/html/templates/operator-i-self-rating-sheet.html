How the reviewer interprets the bar (and the open ambiguity)

Framework §2 contains two slightly different framings:

  1. Per-pillar table: specifies minimum levels per competency. For Pillars 04/05/06/07, several competencies are described as "rest may be 0."
  2. Crystal-clear bar: "at least 15 of 34 competencies at level 2 or higher, NO competencies at level 0."

These two framings are in tension. The crystal-clear bar is the explicit summary line; the per-pillar table is the guidance.

For Cycle 1, the reviewer applies the crystal-clear bar: no 0s anywhere; ≥ 15 at level 2+. Per-pillar minimums are treated as floors above the no-0-anywhere baseline.

Post-Cycle 1: if reviewers found this ambiguity hard to apply, the Framework v1.2 amendment process resolves it. Resolution is NOT part of this Cycle 1 work.

What disqualifies the self-rating

  • Any 0 marked anywhere
  • Fewer than 15 competencies at level 2 or higher
  • Any pillar-specific minimum (in bold above) below the stated floor
  • A self-rating where reviewer's verified column differs by ≥ 2 levels on ≥ 3 competencies — signals the candidate's self-perception is significantly out of calibration. Conditional Pass with mandatory recalibration in Cycle 2 prep.

Honest self-rating guidance for the candidate

The Pioneer Test applies here too. A self-rating that shows all 2s and 3s with no 1s is almost certainly inflated. A real Operator I has 1s — the competencies they know they need to grow into next year. Marking 1s honestly is a positive signal, not a negative one. Marking 3s falsely is disqualifying under Pillar 7 (Economic Reality / honest economics).
