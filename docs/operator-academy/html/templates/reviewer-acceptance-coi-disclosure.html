Reviewer Acceptance + Conflict-of-Interest Disclosure

Filled by each reviewer at the start of each certification cycle they participate in. One form per reviewer per cycle.

Why this form exists: the Bootstrap Reviewer onboarding kit and framework §7 Step 3 require independence. Independence is only meaningful if relationships are disclosed in writing before reviewing begins.

When it's filled: within 72 hours of receiving the reviewer ask. Before the candidate submits any evidence.

Where it's filed: appended to the cycle record file (certification-cycles/cycle-{N}-{candidate}.md) at decision time. Reviewers keep their own copy.

Independence Affirmations

I affirm the following. Check each box. Unchecked boxes mean the affirmation does not yet apply — flag to Academy Lead before reviewing begins.

  • I have read the Bootstrap Reviewer onboarding kit end-to-end.
  • I have read framework §2 (Operator I bar) — or the tier-equivalent section if reviewing a different tier.
  • I have skimmed the Constitution §"Pioneer Test" and Pillars 7 + 8.
  • I will fill my reviewer scorecard independently — no coordination with other reviewers before submission.
  • I will cite specific evidence (timestamp, file, page) for every Pass score I assign.
  • I will apply the Pioneer Test grading rubric to my own scorecard before submitting it.
  • I understand the final cycle decision is the worst of three reviewer decisions, not the average.
  • I understand that an honest Not Yet is the system working as designed.
  • I commit ~10 hours over the cycle's review weeks (typically Weeks 5–6 of a 6-week cycle).
  • I will not discuss this candidate's cycle or evidence with anyone outside the reviewer + Academy Lead group until the decision is delivered.

What this form is NOT

  • Not new doctrine. Operationalizes the existing independence requirement in framework §7 Step 3 and the Bootstrap clause §7.5 (Triple-Reviewer Safeguard).
  • Not a disqualification gate. Most disclosures result in "proceed" or "proceed with memo." Only material, undisclosable conflicts (active financial entanglement, romantic relationship) disqualify.
  • Not negotiable. A reviewer who refuses to disclose cannot review. No exceptions.
