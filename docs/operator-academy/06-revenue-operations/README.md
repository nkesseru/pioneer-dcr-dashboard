## Standard expansion plays

The pillar centers on three standard plays. Every operator should be able to recite these from memory:

### Play A — Multi-location expansion

The customer has 2+ physical locations and is only using PioneerOps at one. Surface this in QBR. ROI = labor visibility across locations + standardized quality. Average uplift: $X (fill in with real Pioneer number).

### Play B — Premium tier upsell

The customer is on the base tier but is using AI features, advanced reporting, or strategic dashboards heavily. They're getting value beyond their price band. Reset at renewal. Average uplift: $X.

### Play C — Add-on module

The customer has a specific operational pain (callouts, supply runs, customer feedback) that a specific module addresses. Offer trial, demonstrate value, convert. Average uplift: $X.

## Pricing principles (Pioneer's stance)

- We don't compete on price. We compete on operator value.
- We don't discount more than 10% without leadership approval.
- We don't sign multi-year discounts without a paid implementation fee or a guaranteed expansion floor.
- We don't promise feature ETAs to close deals.

These are guardrails. The operator's job is to make a great deal feel inevitable to the customer, not to negotiate themselves into bad deals to "keep the account happy."

## Forecasting discipline

- **Honest > generous.** A forecast that's 90% right beats a forecast that's 60% right and inflated.
- **Update weekly.** Forecast staleness is a leading indicator of operator drift.
- **Annotate the change.** When an account moves from "renewal at full" to "renewal at risk," write down what changed.
