# Mock Certification Cycle 1 — April as Operator II Candidate

> **Purpose: validate the certification system.** Not to credential April. This cycle is a **deliberate stress test** of the Framework v1.1 process, conducted by running the entire end-to-end workflow against a real candidate using existing artifacts.
>
> **Authority:** [Operator Certification Framework v1.1](../operator-certification-framework.md) — specifically the new [Section 7.5 Bootstrap Phase Reviewer Provisions](../operator-certification-framework.md#75-bootstrap-phase-reviewer-provisions).
>
> **Voice:** Academy Director, designing the first cycle in detail before executing it.

---

## Why this candidate, why this tier

### Why April

April is Pioneer's executive responsible for day-to-day customer success leadership. She is repeatedly named as a real practitioner in the Academy's foundation lessons:

| Lesson | April's role |
|---|---|
| [02.2 RPLH](../02-cleaning-operations/02-revenue-per-labor-hour.md) | April pulled the Customer Economics card with ACME's owner; reframed margin compression conversation |
| [03.1 MC Diagnostics](../03-pioneerops-platform-mastery/01-mission-control-diagnostics.md) | April's Monday morning Vital Read across 30 Pioneer accounts; catches Riverside's $13 RPLH drop |
| [04.1 AI Operator](../04-ai-operator/01-prompt-patterns.md) | April's Dental Plaza catch — pauses Pillar 8 verification before recommending action on stale data |
| [05.3 QBR Delivery](../05-customer-success/03-qbr-delivery.md) | April's 11-minute conversation that re-priced six customers at Riverside Office Park |

April plausibly **exceeds** the Operator II bar in actual capability. Her practical demonstrations across 2+ years of Pioneer customer success leadership are equivalent to what the framework asks Operator II candidates to produce.

### Why Operator II (not III or Executive Advisor)

If we ran this mock at Operator III or Executive Advisor, we'd test edge-case requirements (mentorship lineage, doctrine contributions). Those are less informative for validating the standard certification system.

Operator II is the **typical graduate** tier. The bar that most certifications will be conducted against for the next 3 years. Validating the system at Operator II surfaces the most operationally relevant gaps.

### What "mock" means here

- April is a real candidate
- The cycle runs every step from Intent to Decision per Framework Section 7
- Findings are documented at each step
- The decision is **provisional**: if the cycle reveals the system works, the mock can be converted to a real certification with April's consent (and Lineage ID `POPS-OP-II-2026-0001` assigned)
- If the cycle reveals significant gaps, the mock is closed without credentialing, and the framework is amended before any real cycle runs

## Reviewer assignments (Bootstrap Phase)

Per [Framework Section 7.5 Bootstrap Provisions](../operator-certification-framework.md#75-bootstrap-phase-reviewer-provisions), a Bootstrap Reviewer satisfies the Secondary Reviewer requirement during Bootstrap Phase.

### Proposed assignments

| Role | Person | Equivalent-capability justification | Conflict-of-interest concern |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Primary Reviewer (trainer/manager equivalent)** | Nick | Founder; senior to candidate; has worked alongside her on cert framework authoring | Low — Nick is the structural manager-equivalent |
| **Bootstrap Secondary Reviewer** | **Kirby** OR **External (TBD)** | Kirby: documented multi-year admin + supply leadership at Pioneer; familiar with foundation lessons | Medium — Kirby works alongside April daily on supply requests; may be too close for "independent" review |
| **Academy Lead (third reviewer + decision per Triple-Reviewer Safeguard)** | Nick | Founder; authority per framework | **HIGH** — Nick is also the Primary Reviewer above |

### Conflict-of-interest finding (immediate)

> **The Bootstrap Triple-Reviewer Safeguard requires three independent ratings. Pioneer's current staff size makes it structurally difficult to find three reviewers who are not interrelated.**

Possible solutions to surface during the mock:

1. **External Bootstrap Secondary.** An experienced customer-success leader from another company (a former B2B SaaS VP CS, or an experienced commercial-cleaning operations executive) reviewed against the equivalent-capability bar. They get nondisclosure access to the evidence pack.
2. **Time-separated review.** Nick fills out Academy Lead form first (per Section 7.5 Safeguard 1), then waits a defined cooling period before filling out Primary Reviewer form on the same evidence. Imperfect; preserves the independence-of-rating spirit.
3. **Substitute a fourth reviewer.** Bring in Mike (admin) as a fourth, treat Mike + Kirby as the two non-Nick reviewers. Nick remains Academy Lead only.

**Recommendation for mock cycle:** Use solution 3 (Mike + Kirby as the two non-Nick reviewers; Nick only as Academy Lead). Document the choice and revisit for real cycles.

## Mock cycle execution plan

The cycle follows Framework Section 7 Steps 1–7, with explicit findings captured per step.

### Step 1 — April Declares Intent

**Tasks:**

- April fills out [`certification-intent.md`](../templates/certification-intent.md) using existing Pioneer materials
- Nick (Academy Lead) reviews intent for schedule decision

**Anticipated findings:**

- **Process gap:** April's "direct manager" field — does Nick fill it? April is executive; she doesn't have a manager in the conventional sense
- **Process gap:** April's "secondary reviewer proposed" — she must nominate someone the Academy lead would approve; with Pioneer's small staff this is constrained
- **Process gap:** April's "Three Honest Failures" section (Intent Section "Self-Declared Readiness"). She will have failures from 2+ years of CS work. Are any of them recorded?

**To execute:** April actually fills out the intent form (this is a real, time-bounded exercise April can do in ~45 minutes if she has the data).

### Step 2 — Evidence Pack Assembly

**Tasks:**

- April assembles the evidence pack per [`certification-evidence-pack.md`](../templates/certification-evidence-pack.md)
- All Operator II tier required items must be present
- Bootstrap-period stricter requirements apply (+20% per Framework Section 7.5 Safeguard 2): 10 Vital Reads instead of 8; 10 QBRs instead of 8; 15 Loop entries instead of 12

**Anticipated findings — Evidence Gaps (high confidence based on Pioneer's current state):**

| Required artifact | April's likely current state | Gap |
|---|---|---|
| Implementation case study | None documented | ✗ **CRITICAL GAP** — would need to be written from memory for an existing Pioneer customer |
| Recorded customer kickoff (45 min) | Probably never recorded | ✗ **CRITICAL GAP** — no archival video |
| Recorded QBR delivery | Probably some delivered; recording status unknown | ⚠️ **LIKELY GAP** — most QBRs are not recorded |
| AI-assisted analytical artifact + prompt log + edit history | April uses AI per the lessons but prompt logs are not archived | ✗ **CRITICAL GAP** — no formal prompt log |
| Executive briefing (15 min, internal panel) | None on record | ✗ **CRITICAL GAP** — needs to be scheduled and delivered |
| Difficult conversation (recording or transcript, anonymized) | Likely exists in raw notes; not formally curated | ⚠️ **LIKELY GAP** — needs anonymization and assembly |
| Customer health model in active weekly use | Pioneer has Customer Economics card (live) as the de facto model | ✓ **AVAILABLE** — counts as health model if framed |
| Self-Sustaining Loop evidence | Likely exists with at least one Pioneer customer; not documented | ⚠️ **LIKELY GAP** — needs documentation |
| Loop entry log (15 entries with 8 closed including verified failures) | Likely informal; not in framework-required format | ✗ **CRITICAL GAP** — would need retrospective construction |
| Churn-risk save documentation | Pioneer probably has at least one over 2 years | ⚠️ **DOCUMENTABLE** but not yet documented |
| Expansion deal documentation | Pioneer's customers have expanded; not Operator-attributable in formal records | ⚠️ **DOCUMENTABLE** with effort |
| Pillar 8 Discontinuity case | The Dental Plaza catch in the AI Operator lesson IS this — well-documented | ✓ **AVAILABLE** — already in lesson |

**Summary: ~50% of the required evidence pack items exist in some form; ~50% would need to be created retroactively or scheduled forward.**

### Step 3 — Independent Reviewer Reviews

**Tasks:**

- Mike + Kirby independently complete [`certification-reviewer-evaluation.md`](../templates/certification-reviewer-evaluation.md)
- Each rates April's evidence pack 0–4 on the 8 framework requirement sections
- Each applies the Pioneer Test
- Each flags Must Not violations
- They do NOT consult each other before submitting

**Anticipated findings — Scoring Gaps:**

| Scoring concern | Anticipated issue |
|---|---|
| **Reviewer calibration** | Mike + Kirby have never graded a certification before. Their first ratings will not be calibrated against each other or against future reviewers. The Triple-Reviewer Safeguard partly addresses, but the first cycle's ratings are a Tier 0 baseline. |
| **Self-rating misalignment** | April's self-rating (per [`certification-self-assessment.md`](../templates/certification-self-assessment.md)) for someone whose capability exceeds Operator II — does she rate level 3 across all 34 competencies? Does that read as honest or as overrating? |
| **The "5 of 8 sections at 3+" pattern** | If all reviewers rate all sections at 4, the rubric provides no differentiation. Does the system need a 5 (exemplary) score for Bootstrap candidates who are functionally above the tier? |
| **Pioneer Test interpretation** | Question 1: "Would Nick teach it this way?" — Nick IS the reviewer. The Pioneer Test was authored partly by April's work. Self-applying the test to a candidate whose work informed the test is meta-recursive. |
| **Conditional Pass vs Pass thresholds** | If 6 of 8 sections clear ≥ 2 but 2 fail (e.g., missing executive briefing recording + missing implementation case study), does that meet Conditional Pass? Or does the framework expect those to be present? |

### Step 4 — Academy Lead Decision

**Tasks:**

- Nick reviews all three reviewer evaluations
- Nick fills out [`certification-decision.md`](../templates/certification-decision.md)
- Nick makes one of four decisions (Pass / Conditional / No Pass — Gap-Driven / No Pass — Disqualifying)

**Anticipated findings — Process Gaps:**

| Process gap | Issue |
|---|---|
| **Self-decision conflict** | Nick is making the certification decision for a person whose work he has been collaborating on for years. The framework does not currently address this conflict beyond the general "not directly involved" rule. The conflict cannot be avoided in Pioneer's current size. |
| **Compensation adjustment** | April is already executive. Framework Section 7 Step 6 says "Compensation adjustment per band." April's compensation is not on the Operator I/II band system. Does the framework's compensation rule apply, or is April exempt? |
| **Lineage ID assignment** | If pass: April gets `POPS-OP-II-2026-0001`. But what about her parallel role as the Pioneer executive who informed the doctrine? Should her Lineage ID reflect that, or treat her as just-an-Operator? |
| **Public review per Safeguard 3** | The decision must be published in the registry with full reviewer rating context (anonymized). The registry is currently empty. Publishing the first entry needs explicit formatting and a registry entry template. |
| **14-day re-review window** | Framework says April can request a re-review within 14 days if she disagrees with the decision. Realistic for the mock? She probably won't disagree. But the process exists structurally. |

### Step 5 — Decision Delivery

**Tasks:**

- Nick delivers the written feedback + reviewer ratings + Pioneer Test outcome
- Candidate-facing letter (per the appended structure in `certification-decision.md`)

**Anticipated findings — Process Gaps:**

- Candidate-facing letter quality: Nick has not written one before. First version will be rough. Template helps but does not substitute for practice.
- Registry update workflow: who actually adds the entry? Manual edit by Nick? Eventually automated. For mock, manual.

### Step 6 — Recognition (if pass)

**Tasks:**

- Title change effective
- Compensation adjustment
- Lineage entry in registry
- Public acknowledgment

**Anticipated findings — Process Gaps:**

- "Title change" for April: she's already executive. What new title? "Operator II — Executive" hybrid?
- "Public acknowledgment in registry": registry is private to Academy. Is there an external announcement? The mock cycle may not warrant one.

### Step 7 — Renewal Schedule

**Tasks:**

- Renewal date set: 12 months from cert date

**Anticipated findings:** Minor. April's first renewal would be 2027-06-11.

## Recommended Framework Amendments (Framework v1.1 → v1.2)

Based on the gaps identified above, the following amendments are recommended for the next framework version. None require authoring new lessons. All emerge from the mock cycle's stress test of v1.1.

| Proposed amendment | Rationale | Effort |
|---|---|---|
| **Add "Bootstrap Reviewer pre-reading checklist"** with specific reading + verification gates | Mike + Kirby readiness gap | 1 hour |
| **Add level-5 ("exemplary") score** to reviewer evaluation rubric for candidates demonstrably above the tier bar | Scoring differentiation for Bootstrap above-tier candidates | 30 min |
| **Add "Self-Rating Honesty Guidance"** section for candidates who exceed the tier | Self-rating coherence for executives | 30 min |
| **Add conflict-of-interest declaration template** for Bootstrap Reviewers | Reviewer Gap mitigation | 30 min |
| **Add Lineage ID issuance owner section** specifying who creates IDs and maintains the counter | Registry workflow gap | 15 min |
| **Add explicit guidance for above-tier candidates** (executives whose work informs Academy doctrine) | Process gap; affects April + future executives | 1 hour |
| **Define a candidate-facing letter template** for first cycles | Process gap mitigation | 30 min |
| **Add "evidence retrospection" guidance** for candidates whose work predates the framework | Evidence gap mitigation | 30 min |

**Total estimated amendment effort: ~6 hours of authoring.** None of these amendments require new lessons; they refine the existing framework based on stress-test findings.

## Mock Cycle Decision Framework

After the mock cycle executes, one of three outcomes:

### Outcome A — Mock validates the system

If the cycle completes with April demonstrably qualifying AND the framework holding (only minor gaps), the mock can be **converted to a real certification** with April's consent. She gets Lineage ID `POPS-OP-II-2026-0001`.

### Outcome B — Mock surfaces fixable gaps

If the cycle reveals fixable framework gaps (most of the items above), the mock **closes without certification**, the framework is amended to v1.2, and the cycle is re-run with the same candidate using the amended framework.

### Outcome C — Mock surfaces unfixable gaps

If the cycle reveals that the framework cannot be made to work without major restructuring (e.g., the Triple-Reviewer Safeguard is fundamentally incompatible with small-company-size at Pioneer), the framework requires v2 rather than v1.2. The mock pauses; doctrine is reconsidered.

**Recommended path:** Plan for Outcome B. Most gaps surfaced above are fixable with the ~6 hours of amendment work identified.

## The Validation Question

The mock cycle's purpose is to answer one question:

> **"Can Framework v1.1 (with Bootstrap Phase Reviewer Provisions) certify a real candidate from intent to decision without structural gaps that prevent completion?"**

If yes — the framework is operationally ready and the first real cycle can begin with the next available candidate.

If no — the framework needs v1.2 amendments based on this cycle's findings, then re-test.
